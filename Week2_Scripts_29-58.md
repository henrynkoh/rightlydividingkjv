## Script 29 – Exodus 20: The Ten Commandments – For Israel or All?

**Title:** Exodus 20 – The Ten Commandments: For Israel or All People? KJV Right Division
**Thumbnail:** "Stone tablets on dark background, 'For Israel or All?' bold white, gold 'Exodus 20 KJV'"

---

Hello, dear friends, and welcome back. Today we open to one of the most well-known passages in all of Scripture.

*"And God spake all these words, saying, I am the LORD thy God, which have brought thee out of the land of Egypt, out of the house of bondage."* — Exodus 20:1-2 KJV

Did you catch that? Before God gave even one commandment, He identified His audience. "I am the LORD thy God, which have brought thee out of Egypt." He was speaking to Israel — the nation He had just delivered from slavery.

**Right Division:** Who received the Ten Commandments? Israel. The very introduction tells us: "thy God, which brought thee out of Egypt." The Gentile nations were never brought out of Egypt. This covenant was made specifically with the nation of Israel at Mount Sinai. Deuteronomy 5:3 confirms: "The LORD made not this covenant with our fathers, but with us, even us, who are all of us here alive this day."

Now, does this mean the moral principles behind the commandments — don't murder, don't steal, don't lie — have no value for us today? Absolutely not. These reflect the holy character of God, which never changes. But as a legal covenant demanding obedience for blessing, the Mosaic Law was given to Israel, not to the Gentile Church.

Paul makes this crystal clear. Galatians 3:17 tells us the Law came 430 years after the promise to Abraham and "cannot disannul" that promise of grace. The Law was added — it was not the original plan for all humanity.

**Application:** Friend, if you've been carrying the weight of the Ten Commandments as your standard of salvation, hear this good news: you live in the age of grace. Your standing before God is not based on your performance of the Law, but on Christ's finished work. Romans 3:28 — "Therefore we conclude that a man is justified by faith without the deeds of the law."

Study the Law to understand God's holiness and Israel's history. But rest in grace for your salvation.

## Script 30 – Galatians 3:19 – Purpose of the Law

**Title:** Why Was the Law Given? Galatians 3:19 KJV Explained
**Thumbnail:** "Arrow pointing to cross, 'Law's Purpose' bold white, 'Galatians 3:19 KJV' gold"

---

Hello again, dear friends, and welcome. Today we answer one of the most important questions in all of theology: why did God give the Law in the first place?

*"Wherefore then serveth the law? It was added because of transgressions, till the seed should come to whom the promise was made."* — Galatians 3:19 KJV

Notice two key words: "added" and "till." The Law was not the eternal plan — it was added. And it was temporary — only "till the seed should come." That seed is Christ Jesus.

**Right Division:** Paul is writing to Gentile believers in Galatia who were being pressured to come under the Mosaic Law. His answer is breathtaking: the Law was never meant to be permanent, and it was never meant to save. It was given for a specific purpose, for a specific people, for a specific time.

What was its purpose? Three things. First, to expose transgression — to show Israel and all mankind that we fall short of God's perfect standard. Romans 3:20 says "by the law is the knowledge of sin." Second, it was a schoolmaster — Galatians 3:24 says the Law was "our schoolmaster to bring us unto Christ." It pointed forward to the One who could actually do what the Law required. Third, it was temporary — "till the seed should come." Once Christ came and fulfilled the Law, its tutoring role was complete.

**Application:** Friend, the Law was never God's final word to humanity — it was a parenthesis pointing to Christ. If you've been trying to earn God's approval through rule-keeping, I want to gently remind you: the seed has come. Christ is here. The tutor has handed you over to the Teacher Himself.

Galatians 3:25 — "But after that faith is come, we are no longer under a schoolmaster." You have graduated from the Law into grace. Walk in that freedom today.

## Script 31 – Romans 6:14 – Not Under Law but Under Grace

**Title:** Romans 6:14 – Not Under Law but Under Grace KJV
**Thumbnail:** "Chain breaking, 'Not Under Law' white bold, 'Romans 6:14 KJV' gold subtitle"

---

Welcome back, friends. Today's verse is one of the clearest declarations in the entire Bible about where you and I stand before God right now.

*"For sin shall not have dominion over you: for ye are not under the law, but under grace."* — Romans 6:14 KJV

Read that again slowly. "Ye are not under the law, but under grace." This is not a suggestion or an aspiration — it is a statement of fact about every believer in this age of grace.

**Right Division:** Paul is writing to the Church at Rome — believers in the dispensation of grace. He is not talking about a future state or a theological ideal. He is describing your present reality, right now, today. You are under grace.

What does "under the law" mean? It means being in a covenant relationship with God where your standing depends on your obedience to His commands. Every failure brings a curse. Every success must be perfect — because James 2:10 tells us that "whosoever shall keep the whole law, and yet offend in one point, he is guilty of all." Being under law is an impossible and exhausting position.

What does "under grace" mean? It means your standing before God is entirely based on what Christ has done, not what you do. His perfect obedience is credited to your account. His death paid your penalty. His resurrection is your life.

And notice what Paul says results from being under grace: "sin shall not have dominion over you." Ironically, it is grace — not law — that gives you power over sin. The Law tells you what not to do but gives you no power to do it. Grace changes your heart from the inside out.

**Application:** Friend, stop trying to live under a covenant that was never meant for you. You are not a child of the Mosaic covenant — you are a child of grace. Let that truth sink deep into your heart today. You are free. Walk in that freedom.

## Script 32 – Colossians 2:14 – Law Nailed to the Cross

**Title:** Colossians 2:14 – The Law Was Nailed to the Cross! KJV Right Division
**Thumbnail:** "Cross with scroll nailed to it, 'Nailed to the Cross' bold white, 'Col 2:14 KJV' gold"

---

Hello, beloved friends. Today we look at one of the most powerful and liberating images in all of Scripture.

*"Blotting out the handwriting of ordinances that was against us, which was contrary to us, and took it out of the way, nailing it to his cross."* — Colossians 2:14 KJV

Can you picture it? Every ordinance, every requirement, every debt we owed to the Law — taken by Christ and nailed to the cross. Gone. Paid. Done.

**Right Division:** The "handwriting of ordinances" refers to the written legal requirements of the Mosaic Law — the very Law that stood against us as a record of our failures. Paul says it was "against us" and "contrary to us." The Law could only condemn — it could never save. But Christ took that entire record and nailed it to His cross.

This is why Paul immediately follows in verse 16 by saying: "Let no man therefore judge you in meat, or in drink, or in respect of an holyday, or of the new moon, or of the sabbath days." Because the Law was nailed to the cross, no one has the right to put you back under its ordinances — not diet laws, not feast days, not sabbath requirements.

The dispensational significance is profound. In the age of the Law, Israel was under the bondage of ordinances. In the age of grace, those ordinances have been blotted out — not by our effort, but by Christ's cross. This is a completed act. Verse 14 uses the past tense: "nailing it." It is done.

**Application:** Friend, are you still carrying ordinances that Christ already nailed to His cross? Perhaps someone has told you that you must observe certain days, eat certain foods, or perform certain rituals to be right with God. Look to the cross. It is finished. The debt is paid. Walk in the glorious freedom of Colossians 2.

This truth should fill your heart with worship today. Let me know in the comments — what did Christ nail to the cross that used to weigh you down? Subscribe and join us every day.

Thank You, Lord Jesus, for the cross that freed us from every ordinance against us. We worship You for that finished work. Amen.

## Script 33 – Hebrews 7:12 – Change of Priesthood = Change of Law

**Title:** Hebrews 7:12 – When the Priesthood Changed, the Law Changed Too | KJV
**Thumbnail:** "Old priest vs Christ as High Priest, 'Priesthood Changed' white bold, 'Heb 7:12 KJV' gold"

---

Welcome, dear friends. Today we look at a verse that many overlook, but it carries enormous implications for how we understand Law and Grace.

*"For the priesthood being changed, there is made of necessity a change also of the law."* — Hebrews 7:12 KJV

This is a logical and theological statement that the writer of Hebrews presents as an undeniable fact: change the priesthood, and the law must change with it.

**Right Division:** The Mosaic Law and the Levitical priesthood were inseparably linked. The entire legal system — sacrifice, atonement, worship — was administered through the tribe of Levi, specifically the family of Aaron. But Christ came from the tribe of Judah, not Levi. So when God appointed Christ as our High Priest — "after the order of Melchisedec" as verse 11 says — it was a declaration that the entire Levitical system had been superseded.

If the priesthood has changed, then the law governing that priesthood has also changed. This is not Paul's radical idea — Hebrews is saying the same thing. The Law of Moses, with all its ordinances, sacrifices, and regulations, was tied to a priesthood that has now been replaced by something infinitely better.

Christ is our High Priest forever. He offered not the blood of bulls and goats, but His own precious blood. He entered not the earthly tabernacle, but heaven itself. He made not a temporary atonement, but an eternal one. The old system has given way to the new — not because it was bad, but because it was a shadow pointing to this glorious reality.

**Application:** Friend, we now have a High Priest who "ever liveth to make intercession" for us — Hebrews 7:25. You do not need a human priest, a religious ritual, or a legal system to approach God. You come boldly, directly, through Christ. That is the privilege of this age of grace.

## Script 34 – Matthew 5:17-18 – Did Jesus Abolish the Law?

**Title:** Matthew 5:17-18 – Did Jesus Abolish the Law? KJV Right Division
**Thumbnail:** "Cross over Torah scroll, 'Fulfilled Not Abolished' white bold, 'Matt 5:17-18 KJV' gold"

---

Hello, friends. Today we address a question that trips up many believers — and we'll answer it with right division.

*"Think not that I am come to destroy the law, or the prophets: I am not come to destroy, but to fulfil. For verily I say unto you, Till heaven and earth pass, one jot or one tittle shall in no wise pass from the law, till all be fulfilled."* — Matthew 5:17-18 KJV

So did Jesus abolish the Law? He says No — He came to fulfil it. But what does that mean for us today?

**Right Division:** Context is everything. Jesus spoke these words during His earthly ministry to Israel, before the cross, before Calvary, before His resurrection. At this point in time, the Law was still fully in effect — and Jesus, as a Jew under the Law, was living perfectly under it.

The keyword is "fulfilled." Jesus came to fulfil — to completely accomplish — everything the Law required and everything it pointed to. The sacrifices? Fulfilled in His death. The priesthood? Fulfilled in His eternal intercession. The holy days? Fulfilled in His resurrection, Pentecost, and coming kingdom. When Christ cried "It is finished" on the cross, He was announcing the completion of the Law's requirements.

This is why Paul can say in Romans 10:4 — "Christ is the end of the law for righteousness to every one that believeth." Not destroyed — ended by fulfillment. A fulfilled contract is not torn up — it is completed. Once a building is built, you don't need the blueprint anymore. The Law was the blueprint; Christ is the building.

**Application:** Friend, Jesus did not destroy the Law — He honored it perfectly and fulfilled it completely, on your behalf. You don't stand before God on your record against the Law — you stand in Christ, who met every requirement. That is the glory of the Gospel.

## Script 35 – Romans 7:4 – Dead to the Law

**Title:** Romans 7:4 – We Are Dead to the Law Through Christ | KJV
**Thumbnail:** "Tombstone with 'Law' written, cross in background, 'Dead to the Law' white bold, 'Rom 7:4 KJV'"

---

Hello, dear friends. Today Paul uses a brilliant illustration to explain your relationship to the Law in this age of grace.

*"Wherefore, my brethren, ye also are become dead to the law by the body of Christ; that ye should be married to another, even to him who is raised from the dead, that we should bring forth fruit unto God."* — Romans 7:4 KJV

Paul uses the image of marriage to explain it. When a husband dies, his wife is free from the marriage law. She can remarry without being called an adulteress. In the same way, we have died to the Law through Christ — and we are now married to the risen Lord.

**Right Division:** This is a profoundly dispensational truth. In the age of the Law, Israel was "married" to the Mosaic covenant. Their spiritual identity was defined by the Law. But through Christ's death — and our union with Him in that death — we have died to the Law. We are no longer bound to that old covenant.

Notice the purpose of this new union: "that we should bring forth fruit unto God." Fruit! Not in the old way of striving under legal obligation, but in the new way of loving union with the risen Christ. Galatians 5:22 — the fruit of the Spirit. This is what grace produces in us — not the cold performance of commands, but the warm fruit of relationship.

Romans 7:6 confirms: "But now we are delivered from the law, that being dead wherein we were held; that we should serve in newness of spirit, and not in the oldness of the letter." The old way was the letter — rules on stone. The new way is the Spirit — life from within.

**Application:** Friend, are you trying to produce fruit through legal effort? You cannot. You were never designed to. You are now married to Christ — and fruit comes naturally in a loving, living relationship. Abide in Him. Know Him. And watch what He produces in and through you.

## Script 36 – Galatians 2:21 – If Righteousness by Law, Christ Died in Vain

**Title:** Galatians 2:21 – If Law Saves, Christ Died for Nothing | KJV
**Thumbnail:** "Empty cross with question mark, 'Did Christ Die in Vain?' white bold, 'Gal 2:21 KJV' gold"

---

Welcome, dear friends. Today Paul makes one of the most sobering statements in all of his writings.

*"I do not frustrate the grace of God: for if righteousness come by the law, then Christ is dead in vain."* — Galatians 2:21 KJV

Let that sink in. If you can achieve righteousness through keeping the Law — if human effort can make you right with God — then the death of Christ was unnecessary. It was a waste. Paul calls it dying "in vain."

**Right Division:** Paul is confronting a serious theological error that was infecting the Galatian churches. Judaizers — teachers who blended Law and Grace — were insisting that Gentile believers must also keep the Mosaic Law to be truly saved. Paul's response is fierce: to add law-keeping to grace is to frustrate — literally, to set aside, to nullify — the grace of God.

This is the most dangerous error in the Church in every age. It takes many forms: you must be baptized to be saved; you must speak in tongues; you must tithe; you must keep the sabbath; you must observe the feasts. All of these, when presented as requirements for salvation or for God's acceptance, are forms of law-keeping that make Christ's death unnecessary.

Either Christ's death was sufficient — fully, completely, eternally sufficient — or it was not enough and we must add something to it. There is no middle ground. Paul says clearly: if law saves, Christ died in vain.

**Application:** Friend, do not frustrate the grace of God. Do not add one single requirement to the finished work of Christ. Believe on the Lord Jesus Christ — His death for your sins, His burial, His resurrection — and thou shalt be saved. That is the complete Gospel. Nothing to add. Nothing to subtract.

## Script 37 – What Is the New Covenant? Jeremiah 31 vs Hebrews 8

**Title:** What Is the New Covenant? Jeremiah 31 vs Hebrews 8 KJV Explained
**Thumbnail:** "Old scroll vs open Bible, 'New Covenant' gold text, dark background, KJV"

---

Hello, beloved friends. Today we explore one of the most beautiful themes in all of Scripture — the New Covenant.

*"Behold, the days come, saith the LORD, that I will make a new covenant with the house of Israel, and with the house of Judah."* — Jeremiah 31:31 KJV

*"For if that first covenant had been faultless, then should no place have been sought for the second."* — Hebrews 8:7 KJV

**Right Division:** Jeremiah 31 was written to Israel — specifically "the house of Israel and the house of Judah." This is a prophecy of Israel's future restoration, when God will write His law on their hearts and be their God in the fullness of the earthly Kingdom.

Hebrews 8 quotes this prophecy and applies it in the context of Christ's superior priesthood. But here is where right division is essential: the New Covenant of Hebrews is specifically with Israel — it says so in verse 8, quoting Jeremiah directly. This is a covenant that will be fully realized in Israel's future kingdom.

So where does the Church fit? The Church — the Body of Christ — participates in the spiritual blessings that flow from Christ's blood. Luke 22:20 — "This cup is the new testament in my blood." We benefit from the new covenant blood of Christ, but we are not the primary recipients of the Jeremiah prophecy, which belongs to Israel's national restoration.

The faultiness of the old covenant was not in God — it was in the people. Hebrews 8:8 says "finding fault with them." The Law was holy and good. But it could not change the heart. The New Covenant does what the Law could never do — it transforms from the inside out.

**Application:** Friend, we live in the blessing of the shed blood of the New Covenant. We have the Holy Spirit, the indwelling presence of God, the forgiveness of sins. This is breathtaking grace. Let it change you from the inside, just as God promised.

Welcome, friends. Today Paul gives us a breathtaking comparison between the old covenant of Law and the new covenant of Grace.

*"For if the ministration of condemnation be glory, much more doth the ministration of righteousness exceed in glory. For even that which was made glorious had no glory in this respect, by reason of the glory that excelleth."* — 2 Corinthians 3:9-10 KJV

Even the Old Covenant had glory. When Moses came down from Sinai, his face shone so brightly that Israel could not look at him. That was real, visible glory. But Paul says that glory was fading — and it was a ministration of condemnation.

**Right Division:** Paul draws a four-way contrast. The Old was written on stones — the New is written on hearts. The Old brought death — the New brings life. The Old was a ministration of condemnation — the New is a ministration of righteousness. The Old was fading glory — the New is surpassing, permanent glory.

This is dispensational truth at its most beautiful. The Law was glorious — it revealed the holiness of God and the standards of His kingdom. But it was designed to fade, to give way, to point forward to something infinitely better. The New Covenant in Christ's blood shines with a glory that doesn't fade because it is rooted in what Christ accomplished — eternal, perfect, unshakeable.

Verse 11 says: "For if that which is done away was glorious, much more that which remaineth is glorious." The old is done away. The new remains. We live in the "remaineth" — the permanent, glorious ministration of righteousness.

**Application:** Friend, you are not living in a fading glory. You are living in the surpassing glory of the New Covenant. Your righteousness doesn't depend on your performance — it flows from Christ's perfect work. That is glory that never fades.

## Script 39 – Sabbath: Saturday or Principle? (Colossians 2:16)

**Title:** Sabbath: Saturday Worship or Grace Principle? Colossians 2:16 KJV
**Thumbnail:** "Calendar with all days lit, 'Every Day is Rest in Christ' white bold, 'Col 2:16 KJV' gold"

---

Hello, dear friends. Today we tackle a topic that causes genuine confusion in the modern church — the Sabbath.

*"Let no man therefore judge you in meat, or in drink, or in respect of an holyday, or of the new moon, or of the sabbath days: Which are a shadow of things to come; but the body is of Christ."* — Colossians 2:16-17 KJV

Notice that Paul groups the sabbath with other Jewish observances — holydays, new moons, dietary laws. And he says these are "a shadow of things to come; but the body is of Christ."

**Right Division:** The Sabbath was given to Israel. Exodus 31:17 is explicit: "It is a sign between me and the children of Israel for ever." Not between God and all humanity — between God and Israel. The Sabbath was a covenant sign identifying Israel as God's set-apart people.

The shadow is the Sabbath — one day of rest pointing forward to something. The body — the substance, the reality — is Christ. Jesus is our rest. Matthew 11:28 — "Come unto me, all ye that labour and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest." We enter into His rest not one day a week, but continuously, by faith.

Hebrews 4:9-10 speaks of a remaining Sabbath rest for the people of God — but it is not Saturday observance. It is the rest of ceasing from our own works, just as God rested from His. It is the rest of trusting in Christ's finished work.

Romans 14:5 — "One man esteemeth one day above another: another esteemeth every day alike. Let every man be fully persuaded in his own mind." Paul allows liberty here. There is no condemnation for those who worship on Saturday or Sunday — but there is also no requirement.

**Application:** Friend, you have entered into a rest that no day of the week can provide. In Christ, every day is a sabbath. Every moment of trusting His finished work is rest. Live in that rest today.

## Script 40 – Dietary Laws: Still Binding? (1 Timothy 4:4-5)

**Title:** Dietary Laws: Still Required? 1 Timothy 4:4-5 KJV Right Division
**Thumbnail:** "Food table with all foods, 'All Food Is Good' white bold, '1 Tim 4:4-5 KJV' gold"

---

Welcome back, friends. Today we answer a practical question: must Christians observe Old Testament dietary laws?

*"For every creature of God is good, and nothing to be refused, if it be received with thanksgiving: For it is sanctified by the word of God and prayer."* — 1 Timothy 4:4-5 KJV

Paul here calls the teaching that certain foods must be refused a doctrine of demons — verse 1. And he declares that every creature of God is good and nothing is to be refused.

**Right Division:** The dietary laws of Leviticus 11 were given to Israel as part of the Mosaic covenant. Clean and unclean animals were covenant markers — they set Israel apart from surrounding nations and pointed to spiritual realities of holiness and separation.

When Christ came, He declared all foods clean. Mark 7:19 — "purging all meats." Acts 10 records God giving Peter a vision of unclean animals and commanding him: "What God hath cleansed, that call not thou common." This was not only about food — it was a powerful statement that the Gentiles, once considered unclean, were now welcomed into God's family through Christ.

Colossians 2:16 echoes this: "Let no man judge you in meat, or in drink." Romans 14:14 — "I know, and am persuaded by the Lord Jesus, that there is nothing unclean of itself." The dietary regulations of Moses are not binding on the Church in this age of grace.

This does not mean we throw health wisdom away. Eating well is stewardship of the body God gave us. But it means you will not stand before God and be judged for eating pork or shellfish. Those covenant markers belonged to Israel and are fulfilled in Christ.

**Application:** Friend, receive your food with thanksgiving. That is the New Covenant approach — gratitude, not religious regulation. Everything God has made is good, and grace sanctifies it.

## Script 41 – Tithing: Law or Grace Giving? (2 Corinthians 9)

**Title:** Tithing: Law Obligation or Grace Giving? 2 Corinthians 9 KJV
**Thumbnail:** "Open hands giving, 'Cheerful Giver Not 10%' white bold, '2 Cor 9 KJV' gold"

---

Hello, dear friends. Today we address a topic that touches every believer's wallet and heart — tithing.

*"Every man according as he purposeth in his heart, so let him give; not grudgingly, or of necessity: for God loveth a cheerful giver."* — 2 Corinthians 9:7 KJV

Not grudgingly. Not of necessity. Cheerfully. This is the New Covenant approach to giving — and it is radically different from the tithe.

**Right Division:** The tithe — giving ten percent — was commanded in the Mosaic Law. Leviticus 27:30, Numbers 18, Deuteronomy 14. It was Israel's system for supporting the Levitical priesthood, funding the temple, and providing for the poor. It was a tax built into the theocratic government of Israel.

Malachi 3:10, the famous "bring ye all the tithes," was spoken to Israel — specifically to the priests who had been robbing God. Applying it directly to the New Covenant Church removes it entirely from its historical and dispensational context.

In the age of grace, Paul's instruction is completely different. First Corinthians 16:2 — "Upon the first day of the week let every one of you lay by him in store, as God hath prospered him." As God has prospered — proportional, voluntary, from the heart.

Second Corinthians 9:6 — "He which soweth sparingly shall reap also sparingly; and he which soweth bountifully shall reap also bountifully." The principle is generosity, not a fixed percentage. Some who love God will give far more than ten percent. Some in genuine need may give less. The motivation is love, not law.

**Application:** Friend, give from your heart, not from a legal obligation. Ask God what He would have you give — and give it joyfully, freely, as a worshipping response to His grace. That kind of giving moves heaven far more than mechanical tithing ever could.

## Script 42 – Circumcision: Required Today? (Galatians 5:2-3)

**Title:** Circumcision Required for Christians? Galatians 5:2-3 KJV Right Division
**Thumbnail:** "Covenant sign crossed out, 'Not Required in Grace' white bold, 'Gal 5:2-3 KJV' gold"

---

Welcome, friends. Today we address a question that was burning in Paul's day — and still matters for how we understand grace.

*"Behold, I Paul say unto you, that if ye be circumcised, Christ shall profit you nothing. For I testify again to every man that is circumcised, that he is a debtor to do the whole law."* — Galatians 5:2-3 KJV

These are strong words from Paul. If you seek circumcision as a religious requirement for salvation or spiritual standing, you are declaring yourself a debtor to keep the entire Law — and Christ profits you nothing in that arrangement.

**Right Division:** Circumcision was the covenant sign of the Abrahamic and Mosaic covenants with Israel. Genesis 17 established it as the physical mark of belonging to God's covenant people. For a Jewish man, circumcision was the entry point into the covenant community.

But we are not in the Mosaic covenant — we are in the dispensation of grace. Galatians 6:15 — "For in Christ Jesus neither circumcision availeth any thing, nor uncircumcision, but a new creature." What matters is not the external mark but the internal transformation — being a new creation in Christ.

Galatians 5:4 follows with the sobering consequence: those who seek circumcision for righteousness have "fallen from grace." They have left the principle of faith and returned to the principle of works.

## Script 57 – Priesthood of All Believers (1 Peter 2:9 vs Old Testament)

**Title:** Priesthood of All Believers – 1 Peter 2:9 vs Old Testament | KJV
**Thumbnail:** "Crowd of people before God, 'All Are Priests' white bold, '1 Peter 2:9 KJV' gold"

---

Hello, beloved friends. Today we look at one of the most revolutionary truths in the New Testament — the priesthood of all believers.

*"But ye are a chosen generation, a royal priesthood, an holy nation, a peculiar people; that ye should shew forth the praises of him who hath called you out of darkness into his marvellous light."* — 1 Peter 2:9 KJV

A royal priesthood. Every believer.

**Right Division:** In the Old Testament, the priesthood was strictly limited. Only the tribe of Levi could serve as priests. Only the family of Aaron could offer sacrifices. Only the High Priest could enter the Holy of Holies — and only once a year, with blood. The distance between ordinary Israelite and the presence of God was structured, mediated, and heavily regulated.

When Christ died on the cross, the veil of the temple was torn from top to bottom — Matthew 27:51. That veil had separated the Holy of Holies from the rest of the temple. Its tearing was God's announcement: the way into His presence is now open to all.

Hebrews 4:16 — "Let us therefore come boldly unto the throne of grace, that we may obtain mercy, and find grace to help in time of need." Boldly. Not trembling at a distance, but boldly. Why? Because we have a High Priest — Christ — who has gone before us and opened the way.

Now every believer is a priest. You do not need a human mediator to access God. You do not need a clergyman to offer your prayers. You come directly, personally, boldly — through Christ alone. First Timothy 2:5 — "For there is one God, and one mediator between God and men, the man Christ Jesus."

**Application:** Friend, you are a priest. You have direct access to the throne of God right now. Use it. Come boldly. Bring your needs, your praise, your intercession — directly to the Father through Christ. That is your privilege in this age of grace.

## Script 58 – Mediator: Moses vs Christ (1 Timothy 2:5)

**Title:** One Mediator: Moses vs Christ – 1 Timothy 2:5 KJV
**Thumbnail:** "Moses with tablets and Christ with cross, 'One Mediator' white bold, '1 Tim 2:5 KJV' gold"

---

Welcome, dear friends. We come to the final message of Week 2, and I want to close with the most glorious truth of all — we have one Mediator, and His name is Jesus.

*"For there is one God, and one mediator between God and men, the man Christ Jesus; Who gave himself a ransom for all, to be testified in due time."* — 1 Timothy 2:5-6 KJV

One mediator. Not Moses. Not Mary. Not the church. Not a priest. Not your pastor. One — the man Christ Jesus.

**Right Division:** In the dispensation of Law, Moses served as a mediator between God and Israel. He received the Law on their behalf, interceded when they sinned, and stood in the gap for the nation. Deuteronomy 5:5 — "I stood between the LORD and you at that time." Moses was a type — a shadow — of the perfect Mediator to come.

But Moses was finite, sinful himself — he needed a sacrifice for his own sin. He could not bear the full weight of God's holiness and human need simultaneously. He was a picture, not the reality.

Christ is the reality. He is the God-Man — fully God, fully man — uniquely qualified to stand between a holy God and sinful humanity. He didn't just deliver God's law — He fulfilled it. He didn't just intercede for sin — He bore sin. He didn't just stand in the gap — He closed it permanently through His own blood.

"Who gave himself a ransom for all" — this is the ground of the mediation. He paid the ransom. The price is settled. The debt is cleared. And "to be testified in due time" — Paul's gospel was the testimony appointed for this age of grace. The one Mediator's work is now proclaimed to all the nations.

**Application:** Friend, you need no earthly mediator. No saint, no priest, no religious system can bring you to God. But you have One who can — and He is alive, risen, interceding for you right now at the right hand of the Father. Come to Him. Bring everything. He is sufficient.
