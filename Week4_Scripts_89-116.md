# Week 4 Scripts (89-116): The Believer's Position & Practice in the Age of Grace

## Day 22

### Script 89 — Romans 5:1 – Justified by Faith, We Have Peace with God | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "Therefore being justified by faith, we have peace with God through our Lord Jesus Christ." — Romans 5:1 KJV

Dear friends, peace with God is not an emotion. It is a legal declaration. The moment you believed the Gospel — that Christ died for your sins, was buried, and rose again — God's court issued its verdict: justified. Not guilty. Righteous. And with that verdict came peace.

**Right Division:** Under the Law, Israel could never have settled peace with God — because the blood of bulls and goats could never permanently take away sins (Hebrews 10:4). The High Priest entered the Holy of Holies once per year, and the veil remained. But Christ's one sacrifice accomplished what no system of law could achieve. Romans 5:1 declares the result for everyone who believes in this age of grace: peace — permanent, legal, unshakeable.

This peace does not fluctuate with your feelings. It does not depend on your daily performance. It was established at the cross and sealed by the resurrection. When God looks at you, He sees the righteousness of His Son. The enmity is gone. The war is over. You are at peace with the God of the universe.

**Application:** When anxiety or guilt tries to rob you of your peace, return to Romans 5:1. Declare it aloud: "I am justified by faith. I have peace with God through Jesus Christ." This is not positive thinking — it is legal reality grounded in the finished work of Christ. Let this peace, which passes all understanding (Philippians 4:7), guard your heart today. Amen.

### Script 90 — Romans 8:28 – All Things Work Together for Good | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "And we know that all things work together for good to them that love God, to them who are the called according to his purpose." — Romans 8:28 KJV

Dear friends, Romans 8:28 is not a promise that all things feel good, look good, or seem good. It is a promise that the God who works all things is working them together — like a master chef combining ingredients, some bitter, some sweet — into something ultimately good for those who love Him.

**Right Division:** The context of Romans 8:28 is the Spirit-led, grace-age believer. The previous verse describes the Spirit interceding for us with groanings that cannot be uttered — meaning God is engaged in your circumstances even when you cannot find words to pray. This is not an Old Testament prosperity promise. This is the grace-age reality of a sovereign God weaving every thread of your life — the painful ones, the confusing ones, the dark ones — into His perfect purpose.

The word "called" points back to God's eternal purpose. He called you. He knew you. He predestined you to be conformed to the image of His Son (verse 29). Your current difficulty is not outside His plan — it is within it. And within His plan, all things work together for good.

**Application:** Write down your current difficulty. Then write Romans 8:28 next to it. God is not absent from that difficulty. He is working. The God who raised Christ from the dead is working your situation together for good. Trust the Chef, not just the ingredients. Amen.

### Script 91 — Romans 8:37 – More Than Conquerors Through Christ | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "Nay, in all these things we are more than conquerors through him that loved us." — Romans 8:37 KJV

Dear friends, Paul lists the battles: tribulation, distress, persecution, famine, nakedness, peril, sword. And then says — in all THESE things, not after them, not around them, but right in the middle of them — we are more than conquerors.

**Right Division:** More than conquerors is a remarkable phrase — the Greek hypernikōmen means super-victorious. Not barely surviving. Not enduring with gritted teeth. Super-victorious. This is the grace-age believer's standing in Christ. Not because of personal strength or spiritual achievement, but "through him that loved us." Christ is the source of the victory. Our role is to receive it by faith and walk in it.

The following verses nail it shut: "Neither death, nor life, nor angels, nor principalities, nor powers, nor things present, nor things to come, nor height, nor depth, nor any other creature, shall be able to separate us from the love of God, which is in Christ Jesus our Lord" (Romans 8:38-39). The list is exhaustive. Every possible enemy is named and defeated.

**Application:** Whatever you are facing today — name it, then say: "In this, I am more than a conqueror through Christ who loved me." The victory is not coming. It is already won. Walk through your trial today as a conqueror, not a victim. Let His love be the ground beneath your feet. Amen.

### Script 92 — Romans 12:1-2 – Present Your Body, Be Transformed | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "I beseech you therefore, brethren, by the mercies of God, that ye present your bodies a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable unto God, which is your reasonable service." — Romans 12:1 KJV

Dear friends, after eleven chapters of doctrine — justification, sanctification, Israel, the Church, grace — Paul draws the line of application with one word: therefore. Because of all that God has done, here is the reasonable response: present yourself to God.

**Right Division:** Under the Law, sacrifices were dead. An animal was slain, consumed. The sacrifice ended in death. But in the grace age, God asks for a living sacrifice — ongoing, daily, voluntary. Your body — the vehicle through which God's life is expressed in the world — offered back to the One who purchased it. This is worship: not a ceremony, but a life.

Verse 2 adds the transformation side: "be not conformed to this world: but be ye transformed by the renewing of your mind." The Greek word for transformed is metamorphoō — metamorphosis. Not external conformity to religious rules, but internal transformation of the mind by the Word of God. The result: "that ye may prove what is that good, and acceptable, and perfect will of God."

**Application:** Every morning before you rise, present yourself to God verbally: "Lord, this body, this mind, this day — I present them to You. Transform me by Your Word. Use me for Your perfect will." This daily act of surrender is your reasonable, logical, Spirit-led worship. Begin now. Amen.

## Day 23

### Script 93 — 1 Corinthians 2:9-10 – The Spirit Reveals Hidden Wisdom | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "But God hath revealed them unto us by his Spirit: for the Spirit searcheth all things, yea, the deep things of God." — 1 Corinthians 2:10 KJV

Dear friends, verse 9 says eye has not seen, ear has not heard, neither has it entered the heart of man — the things God has prepared. The natural mind cannot access them. But verse 10 is the key: God has revealed them to us by His Spirit.

**Right Division:** In the grace age, every believer has the indwelling Holy Spirit (Romans 8:9). This Spirit is not merely a comforter — He is the revealer of divine wisdom. The deep things of God — the mystery of the Body of Christ, the riches of His grace, the eternal purposes hidden before the foundation of the world — are accessible to the Spirit-taught believer with an open Bible.

Paul says in verse 12: "Now we have received, not the spirit of the world, but the spirit which is of God; that we might know the things that are freely given to us of God." Freely given. You do not need a seminary degree or a religious institution to unlock Scripture. You need the Spirit who indwells you and the Word He inspired.

**Application:** Before you read your Bible today, pause and pray: "Holy Spirit, reveal the deep things of God. Open my understanding." Then read Paul's epistles with expectation. The Spirit who searched the depths of God has come to live in you. He will teach you. Trust Him. Amen.

### Script 94 — 1 Corinthians 6:19-20 – Your Body is God's Temple | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "What? know ye not that your body is the temple of the Holy Ghost which is in you, which ye have of God, and ye are not your own? For ye are bought with a price: therefore glorify God in your body." — 1 Corinthians 6:19-20 KJV

Dear friends, in the Old Testament, the Holy Spirit came upon selected individuals for specific tasks, then departed. Under the New Covenant fully revealed through Paul, the Holy Spirit permanently indwells every believer the moment they believe. Your body has become His temple.

**Right Division:** This is not about external religious performance. Paul is correcting the misuse of the body — specifically sexual immorality — by pointing to the indwelling Spirit. In the grace age, the motive for holy living is not the fear of punishment but the reality of His presence. You would not desecrate a temple. You are one.

"Ye are not your own" — this is the most liberating truth in Scripture. You do not belong to sin. You do not belong to the world's standards. You do not belong to your worst impulses. You belong to the One who purchased you with His own blood. That ownership is not bondage — it is the most secure identity you could ever possess.

**Application:** Today, before any decision — what you eat, what you watch, where you go, what you say — pause and remember: my body is His temple. He paid for it. He lives in it. Glorify God in your body today. Not out of obligation, but out of gratitude. Amen.

### Script 95 — 2 Corinthians 5:7 – Walk by Faith, Not by Sight | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "For we walk by faith, not by sight." — 2 Corinthians 5:7 KJV

Dear friends, five words, two contrasts, one complete philosophy of the Christian life. Faith versus sight. The unseen versus the visible. The eternal versus the temporary. Paul lived this verse through shipwrecks, beatings, imprisonment, and rejection — and emerged declaring that the momentary light affliction produces an eternal weight of glory (2 Corinthians 4:17).

**Right Division:** In the Kingdom program, Israel walked by sight — pillar of fire by night, cloud by day, manna on the ground, signs following. The Kingdom age will be equally visible — Christ reigning on David's throne in Jerusalem. But the Church age is the faith age — between the first and second comings of Christ, where we walk by the Word, the Spirit, and the promises, not by visible confirmations.

This is not blind faith. It is faith with an object — the risen Christ and His confirmed Word. We trust what we cannot see because we know Who spoke the promises. Abraham staggered not at the promise of God through unbelief (Romans 4:20) — and he is our model.

**Application:** What circumstance today demands that you trust what you cannot see? Name it. Then choose faith over sight — not because the difficulty is not real, but because the God who promised is more real. Walk by faith. Take one step that only makes sense if God is who He says He is. Amen.

### Script 96 — 2 Corinthians 5:17 – New Creation in Christ | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "Therefore if any man be in Christ, he is a new creature: old things are passed away; behold, all things are become new." — 2 Corinthians 5:17 KJV

Dear friends, the new creation is not a reformation of the old. It is not a better version of what you were before. It is entirely new — a creation ex nihilo, out of nothing, the same creative power that spoke the universe into existence applied to your soul.

**Right Division:** This new creation is the mystery expressed in practice. In Christ, you are not a Gentile sinner cleaned up, not an Israelite made religious, but a new creature belonging to the one new man (Ephesians 2:15). The old identity — sinner, condemned, dead in trespasses — has passed away. A new identity has been given: righteous, accepted, alive in Christ.

Old things passed away. This is positional truth — your standing before God changed the moment you believed. The progressive outworking of this new nature is the rest of the Christian life — learning to live from the inside out, from who you are to what you do, rather than trying to become by doing.

**Application:** Stop identifying with your old nature. The old man is dead — Romans 6:6. You are a new creation. When the old temptations come calling, remind them: that person they're looking for no longer lives here. A new creature has taken up residence. Live from that truth today. Amen.

## Day 24

### Script 97 — Galatians 2:20 – Crucified with Christ, Christ Lives in Me | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "I am crucified with Christ: nevertheless I live; yet not I, but Christ liveth in me: and the life which I now live in the flesh I live by the faith of the Son of God, who loved me, and gave himself for me." — Galatians 2:20 KJV

Dear friends, this is one of the most transformational verses in all of Paul's writings. Four declarations that redefine Christian existence: I am crucified. Nevertheless I live. Not I, but Christ. By the faith of the Son of God who loved me and gave Himself for me.

**Right Division:** Co-crucifixion with Christ — our old man crucified (Romans 6:6), our sin nature judged at the cross — is the positional foundation of grace-age living. You do not have to crucify yourself. You have already been crucified with Him. You died. And yet you live — but now Christ lives His life through your surrendered personality.

"The faith of the Son of God" — some translations read "faith in the Son of God," but the phrase carries the sense of His faithfulness expressed through our faith. We live by trusting the One who is Himself utterly trustworthy. And the final clause anchors it all in love: He loved me individually, personally, specifically. He gave Himself for me.

**Application:** Today's practice: before any decision, any challenge, any temptation — return to Galatians 2:20. "I am crucified. Christ lives in me. I live by His faithfulness. He loves me." Let that four-phrase anchor hold you steady in the storms of the day. Amen.

### Script 98 — Galatians 5:1 – Stand Fast in the Liberty of Christ | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "Stand fast therefore in the liberty wherewith Christ hath made us free, and be not entangled again with the yoke of bondage." — Galatians 5:1 KJV

Dear friends, liberty — real, God-given, Christ-purchased freedom — is not the absence of all authority. It is freedom from the bondage of the Law as a means of justification and the guilt of a system that demanded what it could never enable. Christ made us free. And Paul commands: stand fast in it.

**Right Division:** The Galatians were being pressured by Judaizers to add circumcision and Law-keeping to faith in Christ. Paul's response is fierce: if you submit to circumcision for justification, Christ profits you nothing (verse 2). You sever yourself from grace and fall back into a system of debt. Right division shows us this is the perennial danger — adding religious requirements to the finished work of Christ.

"Entangled again" — the yoke of bondage is always seeking re-entry. Religious performance, guilt-driven service, rule-based spirituality, the constant need to earn what has already been freely given. Paul says: don't go back. You were made free. Stand in it. Resist the pull back to bondage.

**Application:** Identify one area of your life where you are trying to earn God's favor through performance. Then apply Galatians 5:1: that favor is already yours in Christ. Stand fast in the liberty. Serve God freely, from love and gratitude, not from debt and fear. That is grace-age living. Amen.

### Script 99 — Galatians 6:14 – Glory Only in the Cross | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "But God forbid that I should glory, save in the cross of our Lord Jesus Christ, by whom the world is crucified unto me, and I unto the world." — Galatians 6:14 KJV

Dear friends, Paul could have boasted. He was a Pharisee of Pharisees, blameless under the Law (Philippians 3:6), educated under Gamaliel, a Roman citizen, an apostle who received direct revelation from the risen Christ. But he chose one glory: the cross.

**Right Division:** In the grace age, the cross is the center of everything. Not the Law. Not signs and wonders. Not religious achievement. The cross — where Christ bore our sins, absorbed God's wrath, cancelled our debt, and defeated death. By the cross, Paul says, the world is crucified to me and I to the world. The old attraction to worldly approval, worldly achievement, worldly security — crucified. Dead. Gone.

This verse comes after Paul has written extensively about the futility of the Law and the sufficiency of grace. The conclusion is personal: I will glory in nothing else. No spiritual resume. No ministry achievement. Only the cross. Only Christ crucified.

**Application:** What do you tend to glory in? Your spiritual knowledge? Your church attendance? Your moral record? Paul would redirect you: God forbid. Glory in the cross alone. The cross says you had nothing to offer and Christ gave everything. Let that humility and wonder be your daily posture. Amen.

### Script 100 — Ephesians 1:7 – Redemption Through His Blood | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "In whom we have redemption through his blood, the forgiveness of sins, according to the riches of his grace." — Ephesians 1:7 KJV

Dear friends, redemption means to buy back — to purchase freedom for one who was enslaved. We were enslaved to sin, to the Law's condemnation, to death. Christ paid the ransom price with His own blood. And the forgiveness that came with that payment was not a minimum wage of grace — it was "according to the riches of His grace."

**Right Division:** This is present tense — "we HAVE redemption." Not we shall have it. Not we hope for it. We have it now, in Him. The riches of grace are not measured by our need but by God's abundance. And what is the measure of God's riches? Ephesians 3:8 calls them "unsearchable riches of Christ" — beyond calculation, beyond comprehension, beyond comparison.

The blood of Christ accomplished what the entire Levitical sacrificial system foreshadowed but could never achieve: complete, permanent, sufficient redemption. No more sacrifice needed. No more condemnation possible. The price was paid in full by the most precious currency in the universe.

**Application:** When the enemy reminds you of your past sins, respond with Ephesians 1:7: "I have redemption through His blood. My sins are forgiven according to the RICHES of His grace." Not the poverty of grace — the riches. You are lavishly, abundantly, eternally forgiven. Walk in that freedom today. Amen.

### Script 101 — Ephesians 3:20 – Exceeding Abundantly Above All You Ask | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "Now unto him that is able to do exceeding abundantly above all that we ask or think, according to the power that worketh in us." — Ephesians 3:20 KJV

Dear friends, God's ability is not limited to your prayer list or your imagination. He is able to do exceeding abundantly — the Greek is hyperekperissou, beyond all measure, super-abundantly — above all that you ask. And above what you think. Your biggest prayer and your wildest imagination together do not exhaust what He is able to do.

**Right Division:** The key phrase is "according to the power that worketh in US." This is not general divine omnipotence — it is the specific power of the indwelling Spirit in the grace-age believer. The same power that raised Christ from the dead (Ephesians 1:19-20) is working in you. That power does not sit idle. It works in conformity with God's will and according to His perfect purpose.

This verse closes Paul's great prayer of Ephesians 3 — that you would be strengthened with might in the inner man (verse 16), that Christ would dwell in your heart by faith (verse 17), and that you would know the love of Christ which passes knowledge (verse 19). These are the things God is eager to accomplish in you.

**Application:** What is the biggest thing you have asked God for? Now consider that He is able to do exceeding abundantly above even that. Pray big. Believe bigger. Trust the God whose ability surpasses your best imagination, working through His power already dwelling in you. Amen.

### Script 102 — Ephesians 4:4-6 – One Body, One Spirit, One Lord | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "There is one body, and one Spirit, even as ye are called in one hope of your calling; One Lord, one faith, one baptism, One God and Father of all, who is above all, and through all, and in you all." — Ephesians 4:4-6 KJV

Dear friends, Paul's seven "ones" are the unifying foundations of the Body of Christ. In a world that fragments into denominations, competing theologies, and divisive traditions, Paul anchors the Church to seven indivisible realities that transcend all human division.

**Right Division:** One baptism — not water baptism plus Spirit baptism. In this context, one baptism is the Spirit baptism into the Body of Christ (1 Corinthians 12:13) — the single act that places every believer into Christ at salvation. The seven ones describe the grace-age church in its essential, irreducible unity. Division exists in practice — but in Christ, the Body is one.

The one hope of your calling — not earthly but heavenly. Philippians 3:20: our citizenship is in heaven. Colossians 1:5: the hope laid up for you in heaven. The one body is heading to one destination — to be with Christ, conformed to His image, reigning in the ages to come. United in origin, identity, and destiny.

**Application:** Find a believer from a different background today and recognize them as a member of the one body. The seven ones make you family. Doctrinal precision matters — but so does the unity of the Spirit in the bond of peace (verse 3). Walk worthy of your calling. Amen.

### Script 103 — Ephesians 5:25-27 – Christ Loved the Church and Gave Himself | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "Husbands, love your wives, even as Christ also loved the church, and gave himself for it; That he might sanctify and cleanse it with the washing of water by the word." — Ephesians 5:25-26 KJV

Dear friends, Paul uses the greatest love in history as the template for marriage — and in doing so, reveals the depth of Christ's love for His Church. Christ loved the Church. Past tense, accomplished love. He loved it before it was lovable, before it was faithful, before it was anything at all.

**Right Division:** Christ gave Himself for the Church — not because the Church earned it, not because the Church was beautiful, but so that He might present it to Himself glorious, without spot or wrinkle (verse 27). He is not waiting for the Church to become worthy. He is making it worthy through His own work — the washing of water by the Word.

For the grace-age believer, this is the most profound marriage illustration in Scripture: a Bridegroom who loved sacrificially, who gives Himself to sanctify and beautify His Bride, who will present her spotless and glorious. This is the Church's destiny — and your personal destiny as a member of the Body.

**Application:** Whether you are married or single, meditate on Christ's love for you as His Church. He gave Himself for you. He is sanctifying you through His Word. He will present you without spot, without wrinkle, in glorious beauty before the Father. Let that love transform how you love others. Amen.

### Script 104 — Ephesians 6:10-11 – Put on the Full Armor of God | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "Finally, my brethren, be strong in the Lord, and in the power of his might. Put on the whole armour of God, that ye may be able to stand against the wiles of the devil." — Ephesians 6:10-11 KJV

Dear friends, Ephesians ends where any honest look at reality must end — with spiritual warfare. The believer has a position in heavenly places, but the conflict in heavenly places is real. The armor of God is not optional equipment. It is essential for daily survival.

**Right Division:** Notice the armor's description — truth, righteousness, the Gospel of peace, faith, salvation, the Word of God, prayer. These are not moral qualities you achieve — they are weapons Christ provides. The armor is His armor. "Be strong IN THE LORD and in the power of HIS might." The strength is not yours — it is His, available to you by faith.

The warfare is not against flesh and blood (verse 12) — against people, governments, or visible enemies. It is against principalities, powers, rulers of darkness, spiritual wickedness in high places. This is why prayer and the Word are not optional — they are the offensive weapons in a spiritual battle you cannot fight in the flesh.

**Application:** Put on the armor deliberately today. Verbally claim truth against lies. Claim righteousness against condemnation. Claim faith against doubt. Take the sword of the Spirit — open your Bible and speak the Word into your situation. The battle is real. The armor is provided. Stand. Amen.

## Day 26

### Script 105 — Philippians 1:6 – He Who Began a Good Work Will Complete It | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "Being confident of this very thing, that he which hath begun a good work in you will perform it until the day of Jesus Christ." — Philippians 1:6 KJV

Dear friends, the God who started your salvation will finish it. He did not begin a work He lacks the power or intention to complete. From the first moment of faith to the day of Christ's return or your homegoing — He is performing, actively completing, the good work He began in you.

**Right Division:** This is the grace-age guarantee of progressive sanctification and ultimate glorification. Romans 8:30 confirms: "whom he justified, them he also glorified." In God's accounting, your glorification is already done — past tense. The work begun in you is as certain as the work finished at the cross.

Paul writes this from prison, about the Philippians who are also under pressure. The confidence he expresses is not in their consistency — he knows they are imperfect. The confidence is in God who began the work. The initiator is the completer. The author is the finisher. Your spiritual progress does not rest on your faithfulness — it rests on His.

**Application:** Where are you discouraged about your spiritual growth? Apply Philippians 1:6 directly: He who began this good work in me will complete it. You are a work in progress — but the Artist never abandons an unfinished canvas. He will complete what He started. Trust the Completer. Amen.

### Script 106 — Philippians 1:21 – To Live Is Christ, to Die Is Gain | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "For to me to live is Christ, and to die is gain." — Philippians 1:21 KJV

Dear friends, Paul wrote this from a prison cell, facing possible execution. And he did not write it as despair — he wrote it as triumph. If I live, Christ is expressed through this life. If I die, I gain — I am with Christ, which is far better (verse 23). Either way, Christ wins. Either way, I win.

**Right Division:** In the grace age, absence from the body is presence with the Lord — 2 Corinthians 5:8. There is no purgatory, no soul sleep, no intermediate state of suffering. The moment a grace-age believer departs from this body, they are immediately in the presence of Christ. Death for the believer is not loss — it is gain.

This transforms the fear of death entirely. Death is not the enemy of the believer — it is the doorway. To live is Christ — a purpose worth living for. To die is gain — a destiny worth dying for. Paul could not lose either way. Nor can you.

**Application:** Remove the fear of death by replacing it with the truth of Philippians 1:21. Not by denying death's reality, but by seeing through it to the gain. And until that day, let your life be Christ — expressed through your words, your work, your relationships, your witness. To live is Christ. Live it. Amen.

### Script 107 — Philippians 2:5-8 – The Mind of Christ, Humility and Service | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "Let this mind be in you, which was also in Christ Jesus: Who, being in the form of God, thought it not robbery to be equal with God: But made himself of no reputation, and took upon him the form of a servant." — Philippians 2:5-7 KJV

Dear friends, the mind of Christ is not primarily intellectual — it is attitudinal. It is the posture of voluntary humility. Christ possessed everything — equality with God, the worship of angels, the glory of eternity — and He chose the form of a servant, the likeness of sinful flesh, the shame of the cross.

**Right Division:** Paul uses the incarnation — the greatest act of condescension in all of history — to motivate humble service among believers. If the Son of God could empty Himself of His rights, how much more should we lay down our preferences, our pride, our positions for one another? The mind of Christ produces the actions of Christ.

Verse 8 goes to the ultimate: "he humbled himself, and became obedient unto death, even the death of the cross." The cross was not just physical suffering — it was the ultimate humiliation, the ultimate condescension. And God exalted Him (verse 9). Humility precedes exaltation in God's economy, always.

**Application:** Identify one relationship today where pride has built a wall. Apply the mind of Christ: choose the servant's posture. Not weakness — the most powerful Being in the universe chose it. It is strength under control. Humble yourself. God will handle the exaltation in His time. Amen.

### Script 108 — Philippians 4:11-13 – Content in All States, Through Christ | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "I have learned, in whatsoever state I am, therewith to be content. I know both how to be abased, and I know how to abound... I can do all things through Christ which strengtheneth me." — Philippians 4:11-13 KJV

Dear friends, contentment is not a gift — it is a learned skill. Paul says "I have learned." Learning requires time, experience, practice, and often failure. Paul learned contentment through shipwrecks, beatings, hunger, cold, and imprisonment. He is not theorizing — he is reporting from the field.

**Right Division:** The famous verse 13 — "I can do all things through Christ" — is often taken out of context and applied to athletic victories or career goals. In context, it means: I can be content in any state — abased or abounding, full or hungry, free or imprisoned — through Christ who strengthens me. The "all things" is specifically contentment in all circumstances.

This is grace-age maturity — not the prosperity gospel's promise of abundance, not the ascetic's glorification of poverty, but the Spirit-empowered ability to function, rejoice, and serve whether the circumstances are favorable or crushing. Christ is the source of that supernatural steadiness.

**Application:** What state are you in today? Abased or abounding? Either way, Christ is your sufficiency. Practice saying "I can be content in this through Christ who strengthens me." Not resignation — supernatural steadiness rooted in His presence. Learn contentment. It is worth the curriculum. Amen.

## Day 27

### Script 109 — Philippians 4:19 – God Shall Supply All Your Need | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "But my God shall supply all your need according to his riches in glory by Christ Jesus." — Philippians 4:19 KJV

Dear friends, this promise flows from a context of giving. The Philippians gave generously to Paul's ministry in difficult circumstances (verse 16), and Paul's response is this incomparable promise: my God shall supply all YOUR need. The giver receives from the ultimate Giver.

**Right Division:** Notice the measure: "according to his riches in glory." Not according to your faith level, not according to your deserve-ability, not according to the size of your church — according to HIS riches. Ephesians 3:8 calls them "unsearchable riches of Christ." The supply is measured by God's abundance, not your need.

All your need — not some needs, not only spiritual needs, not only needs God considers important enough. All. The God who clothes the lilies and feeds the sparrows (Matthew 6:26-30) cares about every dimension of your need. Physical, emotional, relational, financial, spiritual — all under the umbrella of His sufficient provision.

**Application:** Name a specific need today. Then stand on Philippians 4:19: My God shall supply THIS need according to His riches in glory by Christ Jesus. Not hoping, not wishing — "shall supply." Definitive. Future certain. Give generously when you have opportunity, and trust the God who supplies all need in return. Amen.

### Script 110 — Colossians 1:27 – Christ in You, the Hope of Glory | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "To whom God would make known what is the riches of the glory of this mystery among the Gentiles; which is Christ in you, the hope of glory." — Colossians 1:27 KJV

Dear friends, Paul identifies the supreme mystery in four words: Christ in you. Not Christ over you. Not Christ near you. Not Christ for you — though all of these are gloriously true. Christ IN you. The hope of glory. The indwelling presence of the risen Christ as the hope and guarantee of your glorification.

**Right Division:** This is the mystery expressed at its most personal and intimate. Israel's hope was the Messiah coming TO them externally — reigning on David's throne in Jerusalem. The grace-age mystery is Christ coming INTO us — indwelling every believer through His Spirit, producing His life from the inside out, making our glorification with Him certain.

"The riches of the glory of this mystery" — pile the superlatives: riches. Glory. Mystery. All three applied to one stunning reality: Christ lives in you. You are never alone. You carry the presence of the risen Christ everywhere you go. The boardroom, the hospital, the broken relationship, the sleepless night — Christ in you.

**Application:** Begin every day with this declaration: "Christ is in me. The hope of glory. His life is my resource." Whatever you face today, you do not face it alone. The one who conquered death and sits at the Father's right hand indwells you. Draw on that inexhaustible resource today. Amen.

### Script 111 — Colossians 3:1-3 – Set Your Mind on Things Above | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "If ye then be risen with Christ, seek those things which are above, where Christ sitteth on the right hand of God. Set your affection on things above, not on things on the earth. For ye are dead, and your life is hid with Christ in God." — Colossians 3:1-3 KJV

Dear friends, the "if" of verse 1 is not expressing doubt — in Greek it is a first-class conditional, meaning "since." Since you are risen with Christ — a settled reality — seek things above. The imperative follows from the indicative. What you are determines what you pursue.

**Right Division:** The Church age believer's position is heavenly — seated in heavenly places (Ephesians 2:6). Therefore the Church age believer's perspective should be heavenly. "Set your affection on things above" — the word affection is phronéō, the same word as "mind" in Philippians 2:5. It means to think, to be mentally oriented toward. Set your mental orientation on the eternal.

"Your life is hid with Christ in God" — the security of this hiding place is absolute. Two layers: in Christ, and in God. Nothing can reach you there. The world cannot access it. Satan cannot penetrate it. Even your own failures cannot dislodge it. You are hidden in the safest place in the universe.

**Application:** Identify one earthly thing today that is dominating your mental energy. Now deliberately lift your gaze to Christ at the right hand of God. Set your mind there first, then address the earthly concern from a heavenly perspective. The view from above always clarifies what matters most. Amen.

### Script 112 — Colossians 3:16 – Let the Word of Christ Dwell Richly | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "Let the word of Christ dwell in you richly in all wisdom; teaching and admonishing one another in psalms and hymns and spiritual songs, singing with grace in your hearts to the Lord." — Colossians 3:16 KJV

Dear friends, "let" implies choice and cooperation. The Word of Christ does not force its way into your life — it is welcomed, received, intentionally cultivated. And when it dwells, it dwells richly — not sparsely, not occasionally, not as a weekend supplement to a busy life, but richly, abundantly, in all wisdom.

**Right Division:** This is the grace-age alternative to Mosaic regulation. Israel had the Law written on stone, read publicly once per year. The grace-age believer has the complete, revealed Word of Christ — available always, indwelt by the Spirit who teaches it (1 Corinthians 2:10) — invited to dwell richly within. The internal transforms what external regulation never could.

The natural overflow of richly indwelt Word is community and worship — teaching, admonishing, singing with grace in your hearts. The Word transforms inward, then expresses outward. This is organic discipleship: not programs but the natural overflow of people in whom the Word of Christ dwells richly.

**Application:** Commit to letting the Word dwell more richly this week. Choose one of Paul's epistles. Read one chapter daily, meditatively, letting it settle. Carry a verse through the day. Discuss it with a believer. Sing a hymn that anchors the truth. Richness of the Word produces richness of life. Amen.

### Script 113 — 1 Timothy 2:5 – One God, One Mediator, the Man Christ Jesus | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "For there is one God, and one mediator between God and men, the man Christ Jesus; Who gave himself a ransom for all, to be testified in due time." — 1 Timothy 2:5-6 KJV

Dear friends, one God. One mediator. No committee of saints, no chain of priests, no hierarchy of intercessors between you and God. One. The man Christ Jesus — fully God, fully man — who gave Himself as a ransom for all. He is your direct, living, ever-present access to the Father.

**Right Division:** In Israel's system, the Levitical priesthood mediated between God and the people. Only the High Priest could enter the Holy of Holies, and only once per year. The veil separated man from God. But at the moment of Christ's death, the veil was torn from top to bottom (Matthew 27:51) — from God's side down. The mediation of Christ made the mediation of priests permanently unnecessary.

For the grace-age believer: you come to God directly, in the name of the one Mediator — Christ Jesus. No priestly intercessor. No confession booth. No religious hierarchy. Hebrews 4:16: "Come boldly unto the throne of grace." Boldly. Because the one Mediator has cleared your path with His own blood.

**Application:** The next time you pray, remind yourself: I am coming to the Father through the one Mediator, Christ Jesus. No intermediary required. No waiting period. No appointment needed. Come boldly. Speak freely. He is your Father. Christ has given you access. Use it lavishly today. Amen.

### Script 114 — 2 Timothy 1:12 – I Know Whom I Have Believed | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "For the which cause I also suffer these things: nevertheless I am not ashamed: for I know whom I have believed, and am persuaded that he is able to keep that which I have committed unto him against that day." — 2 Timothy 1:12 KJV

Dear friends, Paul is in his second Roman imprisonment — this time in chains, cold, alone (4:13-16), awaiting execution. From that extreme circumstance, he writes not despair but certainty: I am not ashamed. I know. I am persuaded.

**Right Division:** Three layers of confidence in verse 12: I know (personal knowledge of a Person, not just beliefs), I have believed (committed trust in the risen Christ), I am persuaded (settled conviction beyond the reach of circumstance). This is not theological optimism — it is the bedrock certainty of a man whose entire life has tested and confirmed the faithfulness of God.

"He is able to keep that which I have committed unto him" — my life, my soul, my eternity, committed into His hands and kept by His power. Not kept by my faithfulness — by His. The deposit is safe because the Keeper is faithful, not because the depositor is strong.

**Application:** What have you committed to Christ? Your salvation? Your family? Your future? Add this to it today: I know Whom I have believed. I am persuaded He is able to keep what I have committed. The Keeper is faithful. Your deposit is secure. Rest in that certainty today, whatever the circumstance. Amen.

### Script 115 — 2 Timothy 3:16-17 – All Scripture Given by Inspiration | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "All scripture is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness: That the man of God may be perfect, throughly furnished unto all good works." — 2 Timothy 3:16-17 KJV

Dear friends, "given by inspiration of God" — the Greek is theopneustos, God-breathed. As God breathed life into Adam (Genesis 2:7), He breathed His own life and truth into every word of Scripture. The Bible is not merely a human book reporting on God — it is God's own self-expression through human instruments.

**Right Division:** Paul wrote this to Timothy near the end of his life and ministry. The Word is sufficient — for doctrine (what to believe), reproof (what is wrong), correction (how to get right), instruction in righteousness (how to stay right). Four functions that cover the complete spectrum of the Christian life. No additional revelation needed. No new prophetic word required. The complete, inspired Word is sufficient.

"Throughly furnished unto ALL good works." Not some good works. All. The man of God equipped by the Word needs nothing added. This is why rightly dividing the Word — 2 Timothy 2:15 — is so essential. The inspired Word, rightly divided, produces a complete, equipped, unashamed servant of God.

**Application:** Commit to the sufficiency of Scripture today. Resist the pull to add human tradition, extra-biblical revelation, or current cultural trends as your spiritual authority. Open the Word. It is God-breathed. It is sufficient. It will furnish you for everything you need. Amen.

### Script 116 — Titus 2:11-13 – The Grace of God That Bringeth Salvation | KJV

**KJV Verse:** "For the grace of God that bringeth salvation hath appeared to all men, Teaching us that, denying ungodliness and worldly lusts, we should live soberly, righteously, and godly, in this present world; Looking for that blessed hope, and the glorious appearing of the great God and our Saviour Jesus Christ." — Titus 2:11-13 KJV

Dear friends, this is the perfect summary of the grace age: grace has appeared (past — the cross), grace teaches us (present — holy living), and we look for the blessed hope (future — the glorious appearing). Past grace, present grace, future grace — all in three verses.

**Right Division:** "Hath appeared to ALL men" — not just to Israel, not just to the circumcision. The grace of God is universal in its offer. The cross was for all, the Gospel is for all, the invitation is for all. This is the mystery of the grace age in miniature.

"Teaching us to deny ungodliness" — grace teaches holy living more effectively than law. Not through threat but through transformation. And the motivation: "looking for that blessed hope" — the Rapture, the glorious appearing of Christ. Hope pulls us forward. The expectation of seeing Him face to face reshapes how we live today.

"The blessed hope" — this is the Church's unique anticipation. Not the Kingdom, not the Millennium, but the catching up to meet Christ in the air. We are looking, watching, waiting. And that hope purifies — "every man that hath this hope in him purifieth himself" (1 John 3:3).

**Application:** Live today with the blessed hope. He could come today. Let that reality make you more patient, more loving, more holy, more generous. The grace that saved you is the grace that sustains you and will one day glorify you at His appearing. To God be the glory — great things He hath done. Amen.
