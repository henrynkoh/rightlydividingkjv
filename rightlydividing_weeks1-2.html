Day 1-2: Who Is the Word of Truth For? Context Determines the Recipient

KJV: All scripture is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness. (2 Timothy 3:16)

All Scripture is inspired and profitable — but not all Scripture is directly addressed to you. The Bible contains God's word to Israel, God's word to the Church, God's word to the nations. Rightly dividing means identifying who God is speaking to in each passage.

Example: When God told Noah to build an ark, that was Scripture — inspired, authoritative. But it was not a command to you. The same principle applies throughout Scripture. Israel was told to observe the Sabbath. The Church is not under that covenant.

When you read any verse, ask: Who is God speaking to? When? Under which covenant? This simple discipline transforms Bible reading from confusion into crystal clarity.

Day 1-3: Kingdom Gospel vs Grace Gospel – Matthew 4:23 vs 1 Corinthians 15:1-4

KJV: Moreover, brethren, I declare unto you the gospel which I preached unto you... how that Christ died for our sins according to the scriptures; And that he was buried, and that he rose again the third day. (1 Corinthians 15:1,3-4)

Jesus preached "the gospel of the kingdom" — Matthew 4:23. Paul preached "the gospel of the grace of God" — Acts 20:24. Are these the same gospel? Right division shows us they are distinct.

The Kingdom Gospel announced that the King had come and the Kingdom was at hand — Matthew 3:2. It was for Israel, accompanied by signs and miracles. The Grace Gospel is the finished work of Christ — His death, burial, and resurrection — for the salvation of all who believe, apart from signs and apart from works.

Friend, the Gospel that saves you today is 1 Corinthians 15:1-4. Believe it. Rest in it. It is finished.

Day 1-4: Why So Many Denominations? The Failure to Rightly Divide

KJV: Now I beseech you, brethren, mark them which cause divisions and offences contrary to the doctrine which ye have learned; and avoid them. (Romans 16:17)

Why does Christianity have thousands of denominations? Because for centuries, believers have mixed what God said to Israel with what God says to the Church. When you take Kingdom instructions and apply them to the Grace age, confusion is inevitable.

Some insist on water baptism for salvation — taking Acts 2:38 (Peter to Israel) and applying it to the Church age. Others demand Sabbath observance — taking the Mosaic Law covenant and placing Gentile believers under it. Right division is not division — it is the remedy for division.

When every believer rightly divides, the confusion dissolves. Paul's epistles — Ephesians through Philemon — are your operating instructions for this age of grace.

Day 2-1: Acts 2:38 – "Repent and Be Baptized" – Kingdom or Grace?

KJV: Then Peter said unto them, Repent, and be baptized every one of you in the name of Jesus Christ for the remission of sins, and ye shall receive the gift of the Holy Ghost. (Acts 2:38)

Acts 2:38 is one of the most debated verses in Christianity. Peter commands repentance and water baptism for the remission of sins. Does this apply to us today? Right division answers clearly.

Peter was speaking to Jewish men — Acts 2:5,14 — on the Day of Pentecost, offering the Kingdom to Israel. This is the Kingdom program. The remission of sins through national repentance and baptism is a Kingdom covenant offer to Israel. Compare this to Paul's Gospel — Romans 10:9-10 — believe in your heart, confess with your mouth. No water. No formula. Faith alone.

Acts 2:38 is true Scripture — but it was spoken in the Kingdom context to Israel. Your salvation today rests on 1 Corinthians 15:1-4 and Romans 10:9-10. Faith alone in Christ alone.

Day 2-2: Sermon on the Mount – Law, Grace, or Kingdom Ethics?

KJV: Think not that I am come to destroy the law, or the prophets: I am not come to destroy, but to fulfil. (Matthew 5:17)

The Sermon on the Mount is glorious — but it is often misapplied to the Church. Jesus delivered it to His Jewish disciples as the ethics of the coming Kingdom. He intensified the Law — not to save by works, but to show Israel that no one could meet God's standard apart from grace.

Matthew 5:20 — "Except your righteousness shall exceed the righteousness of the scribes and Pharisees, ye shall in no case enter into the kingdom of heaven." This is Kingdom standard, not Church age justification. For the Church, Romans 3:28 — "a man is justified by faith without the deeds of the law."

Read the Sermon on the Mount and marvel at Christ's perfection. But stand on Paul's Gospel of grace for your righteousness before God.

Day 2-3: John 3:16 – Dispensational Context of "Whosoever Believeth"

KJV: For God so loved the world, that he gave his only begotten Son, that whosoever believeth in him should not perish, but have everlasting life. (John 3:16)

John 3:16 is the most beloved verse in Scripture — and it is absolutely true. But what does "believeth" mean in its context? Jesus spoke these words to Nicodemus, a Jewish ruler, before the cross, before the resurrection, before Paul's revelation of the mystery.

The object of faith is Christ — that never changes. But the full content of the saving Gospel — the death, burial, and resurrection — had not yet been accomplished or revealed. Today we believe the completed Gospel of 1 Corinthians 15. John 3:16 points to the same Saviour, and the same grace expands in Paul's revelation to include all the riches of the mystery.

God loves the world — you — with the full love expressed in Christ's finished work. Believe in Him wholly, as Paul reveals Him fully.

Day 2-4: Hebrews – Written to Jews or to the Church?

KJV: God, who at sundry times and in divers manners spake in time past unto the fathers by the prophets, Hath in these last days spoken unto us by his Son. (Hebrews 1:1-2)

The book of Hebrews is addressed to — Hebrews. Jewish believers who were being pressured to return to the Mosaic system. The entire argument of Hebrews is that Christ is better — better than angels, better than Moses, better than Aaron, better than the Levitical priesthood.

This is vital context. Hebrews 6:4-6 — the warning about falling away — is addressed to Hebrews in a specific dispensational context. Hebrews 10:26 — willful sinning after receiving the knowledge of the truth — similarly has a Kingdom context. These passages do not teach that Church age believers can lose their salvation.

Read Hebrews and marvel at Christ's superiority. But understand its Jewish audience and Kingdom transition context before applying every warning directly to yourself.

Day 3-1: The Abrahamic Covenant – Unconditional Promise to Israel

KJV: And I will establish my covenant between me and thee and thy seed after thee in their generations for an everlasting covenant, to be a God unto thee, and to thy seed after thee. (Genesis 17:7)

The Abrahamic Covenant is the foundation of all God's dealings with Israel. God made an unconditional, unilateral promise to Abraham — land, seed, and blessing. Genesis 15 records God alone passing between the pieces — Abraham was asleep. This covenant did not depend on Abraham's performance.

This covenant is eternal and literal. The land promise — Genesis 15:18 — a specific geographic territory — has never been fully fulfilled. It awaits the Millennial Kingdom when Israel will possess the full extent of the promised land under King Jesus.

God keeps His covenants. His promise to Abraham guarantees Israel's future — and His promise to you in Christ is equally certain. He cannot lie.

Day 3-2: The Mosaic Covenant – Conditional Law for Israel at Sinai

KJV: Now therefore, if ye will obey my voice indeed, and keep my covenant, then ye shall be a peculiar treasure unto me above all people. (Exodus 19:5)

Unlike the Abrahamic Covenant, the Mosaic Covenant was conditional — "if ye will obey." God gave Israel the Law at Sinai — 613 commandments — as the terms of their national relationship with Him. Obedience brought blessing; disobedience brought cursing — Deuteronomy 28.

This covenant was made with Israel at a specific time for a specific purpose. Galatians 3:19 — "It was added because of transgressions, till the seed should come." The Law was never meant to save — it was meant to reveal sin and point to Christ. Galatians 3:24 — "The law was our schoolmaster to bring us unto Christ."

The Law accomplished its purpose. Christ came. Now we are no longer under the schoolmaster — we stand in the grace of God through faith in Christ.

KJV: And thine house and thy kingdom shall be established for ever before thee: thy throne shall be established for ever. (2 Samuel 7:16)

The Davidic Covenant guarantees a literal, future Kingdom. For the Church, we do not wait for an earthly throne — our citizenship is in heaven — Philippians 3:20. But we rejoice that Israel's King is coming.

KJV: Now to him that is of power to stablish you according to my gospel, and the preaching of Jesus Christ, according to the revelation of the mystery, which was kept secret since the world began. (Romans 16:25)

Day 4-2: Romans 3:28 – Justified by Faith Without the Deeds of the Law

KJV: Therefore we conclude that a man is justified by faith without the deeds of the law. (Romans 3:28)

"Without the deeds of the law." Paul could not be clearer. Justification — being declared righteous before God — comes through faith alone, not through works, not through law-keeping, not through sacraments, not through religious performance.

This was the great Reformation battle cry — sola fide, faith alone. But Paul said it 1500 years before the Reformation. Abraham believed God and it was counted to him for righteousness — Romans 4:3 — before circumcision, before the Law, before any religious act. Faith has always been the instrument of justification.

You are justified by faith alone in Christ alone. Not faith plus baptism. Not faith plus obedience. Faith alone. Stand in that freedom today.

Day 4-3: Ephesians 2:8-9 – Grace Through Faith, Not of Works

KJV: For by grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves: it is the gift of God: Not of works, lest any man should boast. (Ephesians 2:8-9)

Three declarations that secure our salvation. First — by grace. Not by merit, not by performance, but by God's unearned, undeserved favor. Second — through faith. Faith is the hand that receives what grace provides. Third — not of works. Works are explicitly excluded from the equation of salvation.

"Lest any man should boast." If salvation involved any human effort, someone could boast. God eliminates boasting entirely by making salvation wholly of grace, wholly received by faith. Verse 10 then shows that good works are the result of salvation — not the cause. We are saved for good works, not by them.

You cannot earn it. You cannot add to it. You can only receive it. That is the beauty of grace.

Day 5-1: Acts – A Transition Book, Not the Church's Handbook

KJV: And as they went through the cities, they delivered them the decrees for to keep, that were ordained of the apostles and elders which were at Jerusalem. (Acts 16:4)

The book of Acts records a transition — from Israel's Kingdom program to the revelation of the mystery through Paul. It is not a normative pattern for the Church age. Acts records water baptisms, speaking in tongues, healing the sick, raising the dead, communal living — all of which were signs authenticating the Kingdom offer to Israel in the transition period.

Taking Acts as the pattern for the Church today leads to chasing sign gifts, demanding tongues as evidence of salvation, and practicing water baptism for remission of sins. Right division reads Acts as history and transition, then turns to Paul's epistles — Romans through Philemon — for the Church's doctrine and practice.

Read Acts for inspiration and history. Live by Paul's epistles for doctrine and practice. That is right division applied.

Day 5-2: Peter vs Paul – Two Apostles, Two Programs

KJV: But contrariwise, when they saw that the gospel of the uncircumcision was committed unto me, as the gospel of the circumcision was unto Peter. (Galatians 2:7)

God gave Peter the gospel of the circumcision — to Israel. God gave Paul the gospel of the uncircumcision — to Gentiles. Two apostles, two distinct commissions, two distinct programs operating simultaneously during the Acts transition period.

This is not contradiction — it is dispensational distinction. Peter's ministry centered on Jerusalem, the Kingdom offer to Israel, signs and wonders, water baptism. Paul's ministry centered on Antioch and beyond, the mystery of the Body of Christ, the Gospel of Grace, Spirit baptism. When you confuse Peter's ministry with Paul's, you confuse yourself.

For this age of grace, follow Paul as he followed Christ — 1 Corinthians 11:1. His epistles are your instruction manual for life in the Body of Christ.

Day 6-1: Dispensations – God's Different Ways of Dealing with Mankind

KJV: That in the dispensation of the fulness of times he might gather together in one all things in Christ, both which are in heaven, and which are on earth; even in him. (Ephesians 1:10)

Dispensation — the Greek word oikonomia — means a stewardship or administration. God has administered His relationship with mankind differently in different ages. Innocence in Eden. Conscience after the Fall. Human government after the Flood. Promise with Abraham. Law with Moses. Grace with Paul. Kingdom to come.

Each dispensation has its own rules, responsibilities, and revelation. Failing to recognize these different administrations is the root of almost every theological error. God is consistent in His character — holy, loving, faithful — but His mode of administration changes according to His sovereign purpose.

We live in the dispensation of grace — the most favorable age in all of history. No sacrifices, no temple, no priestly mediator. Direct access to God through Christ by the Spirit. Be grateful.

Under the Law, blessing depended on obedience — Deuteronomy 28. Disobedience meant curse. The relationship with God was conditional on performance. But in the age of grace, God says "I will never leave thee, nor forsake thee" — unconditionally. The Greek is emphatic: "I will never, no not ever, no not ever forsake you."

Paul confirms this in Romans 8:38-39 — neither death, nor life, nor angels, nor principalities, nor powers, nor things present, nor things to come, nor height, nor depth, nor any other creature shall be able to separate us from the love of God. The list is exhaustive. Nothing is left out. You are eternally secure in Christ.

God will never leave you. That is not a feeling — it is a promise. Rest in His presence today, especially if circumstances make His nearness hard to feel.

Day 14-1: The Law's Shadows vs Grace's Reality – Colossians 2:17

KJV: Which are a shadow of things to come; but the body is of Christ. (Colossians 2:17)

Shadows versus substance. The entire Levitical system — sacrifices, feasts, new moons, Sabbaths — were shadows cast by the coming reality. A shadow has the shape of the real thing but no substance of its own. When the real thing arrives, the shadow becomes unnecessary.

Christ is the substance. The Passover lamb was a shadow — Christ is the Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world. The Day of Atonement was a shadow — Christ is the once-for-all High Priest who offered Himself. The tabernacle was a shadow — Christ is the true Tabernacle in whom God dwells among men. Every shadow points to Him.

You have the substance — Christ Himself. Do not return to the shadows. Celebrate what the shadows pointed to — the full, glorious, perfect reality of Jesus Christ.

Day 14-2: The Feasts of Israel – Fulfilled in Christ

KJV: For even Christ our passover is sacrificed for us: Therefore let us keep the feast, not with old leaven, neither with the leaven of malice and wickedness; but with the unleavened bread of sincerity and truth. (1 Corinthians 5:7-8)

The seven feasts of Israel — Passover, Unleavened Bread, Firstfruits, Pentecost, Trumpets, Atonement, Tabernacles — were the prophetic calendar of redemption. The spring feasts were fulfilled at Christ's first coming: Passover at His crucifixion, Unleavened Bread in His sinless burial, Firstfruits in His resurrection, Pentecost in the Spirit's coming.

The fall feasts — Trumpets, Atonement, Tabernacles — await fulfillment at Christ's second coming. Trumpets in the Rapture or the Second Coming. Atonement in Israel's national repentance. Tabernacles in the Millennial Kingdom. God is the Author of a perfect calendar.

Every feast points to Christ — crucified, buried, risen, coming again. Celebrate the One every feast was designed to announce.

Day 14-3: The Levitical Priesthood vs Christ Our High Priest (Hebrews 7:25)

KJV: Wherefore he is able also to save them to the uttermost that come unto God by him, seeing he ever liveth to make intercession for them. (Hebrews 7:25)

The Levitical priests served daily — offering sacrifices that could never finally take away sin — Hebrews 10:11. They died and were replaced. Their work was never finished. But Christ, our High Priest after the order of Melchizedek — eternal, without beginning or end of days — offered one sacrifice for sins forever and sat down — Hebrews 10:12.

He ever liveth to make intercession. Right now, Jesus is at the right hand of the Father, interceding for you by name. Not a rotating priesthood, not an earthly mediator, not a human representative — the eternal Son of God, presenting His own blood as the basis for your access to the Father.

You need no human priest to access God. You have the greatest High Priest — eternal, perfect, interceding for you this very moment. Come boldly to the throne of grace — Hebrews 4:16.

Day 14-4: One Sacrifice for Sins Forever – Hebrews 10:12

KJV: But this man, after he had offered one sacrifice for sins for ever, sat down on the right hand of God. (Hebrews 10:12)

One sacrifice. For sins. For ever. Sat down. Every word matters. The Levitical priests never sat down — there were no seats in the tabernacle — because their work was never complete. But Christ sat down. His work is finished. One sacrifice accomplishes what a million animal sacrifices could not — the permanent, complete, eternal removal of sin's guilt for all who believe.

"For ever" — not for a season, not until you sin again, not until the next confession. For ever. Your sins — past, present, and future — were all future when Christ died. He bore them all, once, for ever. This is the glorious Gospel of grace.

The sacrifice is made. The work is done. The Priest is seated. Your sins are forgiven — for ever. Rest in that finished work with all your heart today and every day.
