Post 261 · Day 64-1: 1 Thessalonians 4:16-17 – The Rapture: Caught Up to Meet the Lord

Who This Is For: Grace-age believers in the Body of Christ seeking to understand prophecy rightly divided from Paul's epistles and the whole counsel of God.
“For the Lord himself shall descend from heaven with a shout, with the voice of the archangel, and with the trump of God: and the dead in Christ shall rise first: Then we which are alive and remain shall be caught up together with them in the clouds, to meet the Lord in the air: and so shall we ever be with the Lord.” — 1 Thessalonians 4:16-17 KJV

The Rapture — harpazo, 'caught up' — is the blessed hope of the Body of Christ. The Lord descends with a shout, the dead in Christ rise, and living believers are transformed and caught up to meet Him in the air. This is not the Second Coming to earth but a catching away before the Tribulation's fury.

Right Division: Right Division: The Rapture is a mystery revealed to Paul (1 Corinthians 15:51-52) — not found in Old Testament prophecy. The Second Coming described in Zechariah 14 and Revelation 19 has Christ's feet touching the Mount of Olives to establish Israel's Kingdom. The Rapture has Christ meeting believers in the air. Two distinct events for two distinct programs: the Body of Christ is caught up; Israel's Kingdom program is completed through the Tribulation and Second Coming.

Application: Live today with the Rapture in view. Not with anxiety but with anticipation. 'Even so, come, Lord Jesus' (Revelation 22:20). Let the imminence of His return give urgency to your witness and peace in your trials. He could come today.

Post 262 · Day 64-2: 1 Corinthians 15:51-52 – In a Moment, in the Twinkling of an Eye

Who This Is For: Grace-age believers in the Body of Christ seeking to understand prophecy rightly divided from Paul's epistles and the whole counsel of God.
“Behold, I shew you a mystery; We shall not all sleep, but we shall all be changed, In a moment, in the twinkling of an eye, at the last trump: for the trumpet shall sound, and the dead shall be raised incorruptible, and we shall be changed.” — 1 Corinthians 15:51-52 KJV

Paul calls the Rapture a mystery — not previously revealed. The transformation is instantaneous: a twinkling of an eye, the briefest measurable moment. The corruptible puts on incorruption; the mortal puts on immortality. Death is swallowed up in victory.

Right Division: Right Division: 'I shew you a mystery' establishes that this event — living believers transformed without dying — had never been revealed before Paul. The Old Testament prophets wrote of resurrection at the end of the age (Daniel 12:2) but not of a mid-history rapture of a heavenly people. This mystery belongs exclusively to the Body of Christ program. The trump here is not the seventh trumpet of Revelation (Tribulation period) but Paul's last trump signaling the age of grace's completion.

Application: Your body will be changed — glorified, incorruptible, fit for eternity in Christ's presence. Let this certainty affect how you treat your body now: not with contempt (it will be glorified) but with stewardship (it is the Spirit's temple). Live as someone who is being prepared for transformation.

Post 265 · Day 65-1: Daniel 9:27 – The Seventieth Week of Daniel

Who This Is For: Grace-age believers in the Body of Christ seeking to understand prophecy rightly divided from Paul's epistles and the whole counsel of God.

Daniel's seventy weeks are the prophetic timeline of Israel's national dealings with God. The seventieth week — seven years — remains unfulfilled. In its midst, the Antichrist breaks his covenant with Israel and ends temple worship. This is the Tribulation, the time of Jacob's trouble (Jeremiah 30:7).

Right Division: Right Division: Daniel 9:24 says the seventy weeks are determined upon Daniel's people (Israel) and Daniel's holy city (Jerusalem) — not upon the Body of Christ. The Church age is a parenthesis in the prophetic calendar. When the Rapture removes the Body of Christ, the seventieth week resumes for Israel. Right division keeps this distinction clear: the Tribulation is Israel's program, not the Body of Christ's. We do not go through the seventieth week — we are raptured before it.

Application: Daniel 9:27 is not a verse about your future — it is a verse about Israel's future. But understanding it correctly protects you from fear. You will not face the seventieth week's judgments. The blessed hope removes you before it begins. Let this clarity produce gratitude, not anxiety.

Confusing them creates theological chaos; rightly dividing them brings prophetic clarity.

Application: Zechariah 14:4 is a promise to Israel that has never been fulfilled — it is still future. God keeps His promises to Israel. The Thessalonian believers had turned from idols to serve the living God — and they were waiting for His Son from heaven. The word 'delivered' is past tense: the deliverance from coming wrath is already accomplished. Christ's death and resurrection is the basis of our certain deliverance.

Right Division: Right Division: 'The wrath to come' is the Tribulation period — the Day of the Lord's wrath poured out on a Christ-rejecting world. The Body of Christ is not appointed to wrath (1 Thessalonians 5:9). The pretribulation Rapture is the mechanism of this deliverance: we are caught up before the wrath begins. This is not escape theology — it is covenant theology. God promised deliverance through Christ's work, and He will deliver.
