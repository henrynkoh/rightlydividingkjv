Post 233 · Day 57-1: Acts 9:15 – Paul: A Chosen Vessel to the Gentiles

Who This Is For: Grace-age believers in the Body of Christ seeking to understand and live Paul's Gospel rightly divided.
“He is a chosen vessel unto me, to bear my name before the Gentiles, and kings, and the children of Israel.” — Acts 9:15 KJV

Before Paul ever preached a word, God declared him a chosen vessel. The instrument was selected before the ministry began. God does not recruit volunteers and then equip them — He chooses His instruments and fashions them for the work.

Right Division: Right Division: Paul's apostleship to the Gentiles is not a continuation of the Twelve's ministry to Israel. Jesus sent the Twelve to the lost sheep of the house of Israel (Matthew 10:5-6). Paul was specifically separated to carry the name of Christ to Gentiles, kings, and Israel — in that order. This is the dispensational shift: from a Kingdom program centered on Israel to a Mystery program centered on the Body of Christ, administered through Paul's unique apostleship.

Application: Ask God today what vessel He is fashioning you to be. You do not choose your gift — God places members in the body as it pleases Him (1 Corinthians 12:18). Yield yourself as a vessel for His use, sanctified and meet for the Master's service (2 Timothy 2:21).

Post 234 · Day 57-2: Romans 1:16 – The Gospel: God's Power to Salvation

Who This Is For: Grace-age believers in the Body of Christ seeking to understand and live Paul's Gospel rightly divided.
“For I am not ashamed of the gospel of Christ: for it is the power of God unto salvation to every one that believeth; to the Jew first, and also to the Greek.” — Romans 1:16 KJV

Paul declares he is not ashamed of the Gospel. This is not a casual statement — Paul suffered imprisonment, beatings, and rejection for this message. The power he boasts in is not his own eloquence or strategy, but the Gospel itself: God's power unto salvation.

Right Division: Right Division: 'To the Jew first, and also to the Greek' describes the historical order of the Gospel's proclamation, not a permanent hierarchy. During the Acts period, Paul followed a pattern of beginning in synagogues. But Romans 1:16 establishes that the same Gospel now saves both Jew and Greek equally — no distinction in the grace age (Romans 3:22-23). The 'mystery' (Romans 16:25) is that Gentiles now receive the same salvation on identical terms.

Application: Share the Gospel this week with someone who does not know Christ. Not because you have to, but because you are not ashamed. The same power that raised Christ from the dead is resident in the simple proclamation: Christ died for our sins, was buried, and rose again (1 Corinthians 15:3-4).

Post 235 · Day 57-3: 1 Corinthians 9:16 – Woe If I Preach Not the Gospel

Who This Is For: Grace-age believers in the Body of Christ seeking to understand and live Paul's Gospel rightly divided.
“For though I preach the gospel, I have nothing to glory of: for necessity is laid upon me; yea, woe is unto me, if I preach not the gospel!” — 1 Corinthians 9:16 KJV

Paul strips away any sense of personal merit from his preaching. He does not glory in it — it is a stewardship, not an achievement. The woe is not a legal threat but the recognition that his entire purpose would be unfulfilled if he withheld the Gospel.

Right Division: Right Division: Paul's compulsion to preach differs from the Great Commission's command structure. The Twelve were commanded to go and make disciples. Paul was internally compelled by the grace given to him (Ephesians 3:8) — entrusted with a dispensation (1 Corinthians 9:17). The grace-age minister is not driven by external mandate alone but by internal necessity born of understanding what the Gospel is and what it accomplishes.

Application: Cultivate a sense of inner necessity for sharing the Gospel. Read 1 Corinthians 15:1-4 aloud daily this week. Let the weight of the message — the death, burial, and resurrection of the Son of God for your sins — become so real that not sharing it seems unnatural.

Post 236 · Day 57-4: 1 Corinthians 9:22 – All Things to All Men

Who This Is For: Grace-age believers in the Body of Christ seeking to understand and live Paul's Gospel rightly divided.
“I am made all things to all men, that I might by all means save some.” — 1 Corinthians 9:22 KJV

Paul's flexibility was not compromise — it was contextualization in service of an unchanging Gospel. He became weak to the weak, a Jew to Jews, under law to those under law — not because he changed his message, but because he adapted his manner to connect with each person's frame of reference.

Right Division: Right Division: Paul's principle is grace-age evangelism at its finest. He never changed the Gospel — Christ crucified for sins (1 Corinthians 2:2). But he changed his approach. To the philosophical Greeks he quoted their own poets (Acts 17:28). To Jews he opened the scriptures. To Gentiles he preached the living God who made all things. This is missional wisdom: fixed message, flexible method.

Application: Identify someone in your life who needs the Gospel. Ask God how to communicate it in a way that speaks to their specific situation — their fears, their worldview, their background. The message never changes; your approach can.

The ministry of reconciliation is announcing what God has already done, not calling people to a process of earning God's favor.

Who This Is For: Grace-age believers in the Body of Christ seeking to understand and live Paul's Gospel rightly divided.

Paul draws a sharp distinction: his Gospel did not come through human tradition or apostolic succession. He received it directly from the risen, glorified Christ. This is the foundation of Pauline authority — and the reason his epistles carry divine weight for the grace-age believer.

Right Division: Right Division: This verse is crucial for right division. The Gospel Paul preached — the mystery hidden from ages (Romans 16:25) — was not available to the Twelve before the cross. They did not preach justification by faith without works. They preached the Kingdom. It was the risen Christ, after His ascension, who revealed to Paul the mystery of the Body of Christ and the grace gospel. This is why we follow Paul's epistles as our primary doctrinal authority for this age.

Application: Read Galatians 1:11-2:10 this week in one sitting. Notice how carefully Paul establishes the independent divine origin of his Gospel. Your confidence in Paul's epistles should rest not on church tradition but on this claim: this message came directly from Jesus Christ.

Post 242 · Day 59-2: 1 Timothy 2:7 – Paul: Ordained Preacher and Apostle

Who This Is For: Grace-age believers in the Body of Christ seeking to understand and live Paul's Gospel rightly divided.
“Whereunto I am ordained a preacher, and an apostle, (I speak the truth in Christ, and lie not;) a teacher of the Gentiles in faith and verity.” — 1 Timothy 2:7 KJV

Paul's triple title — preacher, apostle, teacher — defines his ministry office. He adds the unusual parenthetical 'I speak the truth in Christ, and lie not' because his apostleship to Gentiles was so radical that it required divine attestation. The very fact that a former persecutor of the Church was made its apostle demands this kind of extraordinary claim.

Right Division: Right Division: Paul is the apostle to the Gentiles — our apostle. The Twelve were apostles to Israel, sent to the circumcision (Galatians 2:7-9). Peter, James, and John had the gospel of the circumcision; Paul had the gospel of the uncircumcision. Right division keeps these apostleships separate and assigns us to Paul's teaching as our doctrinal foundation for the grace age.

Application: Look at your own spiritual formation: are you primarily shaped by Matthew through John, or by Romans through Philemon? Both are Scripture, but Paul's epistles are our specific instruction as members of the Body of Christ. Commit to reading one Pauline epistle completely this week.

Post 245 · Day 60-1: 1 Corinthians 1:17-18 – Not to Baptize But to Preach the Cross

Who This Is For: Grace-age believers in the Body of Christ seeking to understand and live Paul's Gospel rightly divided.
“For Christ sent me not to baptize, but to preach the gospel: not with wisdom of words, lest the cross of Christ should be made of none effect. For the preaching of the cross is to them that perish foolishness; but unto us which are saved it is the power of God.” — 1 Corinthians 1:17-18 KJV

Paul explicitly distinguishes his primary commission from water baptism. This does not eliminate baptism but places it clearly subordinate to the Gospel proclamation. The cross — and its simple proclamation — is the power of God. Eloquence and wisdom of words can actually hinder it by directing attention from the cross to the preacher.

Right Division: Right Division: This verse is a key marker in the transition between dispensations. In Acts 2:38, water baptism was commanded for remission of sins under the Kingdom proclamation. In Paul's epistles, he minimizes water baptism (he barely remembers who he baptized) because his commission centers on the grace Gospel. The Body of Christ is identified by Spirit baptism (1 Corinthians 12:13), not water. Right division clarifies what belongs to which program.

Application: When sharing the Gospel, resist the temptation to add conditions, rituals, or performances. The cross is sufficient. 'Christ died for our sins, was buried, and rose again' (1 Corinthians 15:3-4). Preach that — and trust the power of God to do what no human wisdom can accomplish.

Post 251 · Day 61-3: 1 Thessalonians 2:4 – Approved of God to Be Put in Trust

Who This Is For: Grace-age believers in the Body of Christ seeking to understand and live Paul's Gospel rightly divided.
“But as we were allowed of God to be put in trust with the gospel, even so we speak; not as pleasing men, but as pleasing God, which trieth our hearts.” — 1 Thessalonians 2:4 KJV

The Gospel is a trust — entrusted to those approved by God. And because God is the One who entrusted it, He is the audience whose approval matters. Pleasing men with a softened, adjusted, people-friendly Gospel betrays the trust. Pleasing God means delivering the message accurately, regardless of reception.

Right Division: Right Division: This verse defines the grace-age minister's accountability structure. Under Law, Israel's leaders were accountable to the Mosaic covenant and its visible blessings and curses. The grace-age minister is accountable to the God who tries the heart — the unseen, internal audit of motive and faithfulness. This is a higher and more demanding standard, not because God is harsher but because He sees everything.

Application: Examine your motives in spiritual conversations: are you telling people what they want to hear or what they need to hear? The Gospel includes both grace and truth. This week, don't soften a hard truth that someone in your life needs to hear. Trust God with their response.
Prayer: Lord, thank You for Your Word rightly divided. Help us to study, understand, and live what You have revealed through the Apostle Paul for this age of grace. Give us boldness to share and faithfulness to guard what has been committed to our trust. In Christ's name. Amen.

Post 252 · Day 61-4: Romans 15:20 – Striving to Preach Where Christ Not Named

Who This Is For: Grace-age believers in the Body of Christ seeking to understand and live Paul's Gospel rightly divided.
“Yea, so have I strived to preach the gospel, not where Christ was named, lest I should build upon another man's foundation.” — Romans 15:20 KJV

Paul's missionary ambition was frontier evangelism: places where no one had yet carried the name of Christ. He did not compete with other ministers or retread covered ground. He pressed into unreached territory with the Gospel, building his own foundation on Christ rather than adding floors to someone else's building.

Right Division: Right Division: Paul's strategy reveals the grace-age missional imperative. He kept moving — Antioch to Galatia, Galatia to Europe, Europe toward Spain. The grace Gospel is for all nations simultaneously, not sequentially through Israel. This outward, Gentile-reaching, boundary-crossing impulse defines the grace-age mission. We are not waiting for Israel to be converted first — we go to all peoples now.

Application: Think beyond your existing community: is there a person, neighborhood, or people group in your sphere who has not heard the Gospel because no one has ever gone there? Ask God to give you Paul's frontier mentality — not just deepening where the Gospel already is, but reaching where it has not yet gone.
Prayer: Lord, thank You for Your Word rightly divided. Help us to study, understand, and live what You have revealed through the Apostle Paul for this age of grace. Give us boldness to share and faithfulness to guard what has been committed to our trust. In Christ's name. Amen.

Post 253 · Day 62-1: 1 Corinthians 3:6-9 – Paul Planted, God Gave Increase

Who This Is For: Grace-age believers in the Body of Christ seeking to understand and live Paul's Gospel rightly divided.
“I have planted, Apollos watered; but God gave the increase. So then neither is he that planteth any thing, neither he that watereth; but God that giveth the increase.” — 1 Corinthians 3:6-7 KJV

Ministry is collaborative and Spirit-dependent. Paul planted, Apollos watered — different roles, different seasons, one result that belongs to God. Neither the planter nor the waterer owns the harvest. The increase is God's gift. This liberates ministers from competition and self-promotion.

Right Division: Right Division: The Body of Christ functions as a unit with diverse members, each contributing differently (1 Corinthians 12). Some plant, some water, some harvest. No role is more essential than another — 'God gave the increase' means the result belongs to no human. Grace-age ministry is team-based and God-dependent, not individual-hero-dependent.

Application: Who has 'watered' what you 'planted' in someone's spiritual life? Give them thanks today. And identify someone else's 'planting' that you can water — follow up with someone who has heard the Gospel but not yet believed. God gives the increase when we each do our part faithfully.
Prayer: Lord, thank You for Your Word rightly divided. Help us to study, understand, and live what You have revealed through the Apostle Paul for this age of grace. Give us boldness to share and faithfulness to guard what has been committed to our trust. In Christ's name. Amen.

Post 254 · Day 62-2: 2 Corinthians 6:1-2 – Workers Together With Him

Who This Is For: Grace-age believers in the Body of Christ seeking to understand and live Paul's Gospel rightly divided.
“We then, as workers together with him, beseech you also that ye receive not the grace of God in vain. (For he saith, I have heard thee in a time accepted, and in the day of salvation have I succoured thee: behold, now is the accepted time; behold, now is the day of salvation.)” — 2 Corinthians 6:1-2 KJV

Workers together with God — the most staggering job description in the universe. The grace-age believer is not a spectator but a co-laborer with the God who created everything. And the urgency is real: now is the accepted time. The offer of grace is present-tense.

Right Division: Right Division: 'Now is the day of salvation' defines the grace age. In the Kingdom program, salvation had strong future-oriented elements — enduring to the end (Matthew 24:13), the Kingdom not yet established. In the grace age, salvation is complete at the moment of faith: 'ye are saved' (Ephesians 2:8). The day of salvation is now, not a future kingdom entry. This urgency belongs to the present dispensation.

Application: Do not receive the grace of God in vain — do not live as though the gift changes nothing. You are a worker together with God. Show up to work today: in your family, your workplace, your neighborhood. God is working; be found working with Him.

Post 255 · Day 62-3: Philippians 4:3 – Fellow Labourers in the Gospel

Who This Is For: Grace-age believers in the Body of Christ seeking to understand and live Paul's Gospel rightly divided.
“And I intreat thee also, true yokefellow, help those women which laboured with me in the gospel, with Clement also, and with other my fellowlabourers, whose names are in the book of life.” — Philippians 4:3 KJV

Paul's ministry was not a solo endeavor. He names specific co-laborers — women and men — whose names are in the book of life. The grace-age church is a team. No one is too insignificant to be named; those who labored in the Gospel are permanently recorded in God's book.

Right Division: Right Division: The grace-age Body of Christ is not hierarchical in the Mosaic sense — there is no Levitical priesthood, no gendered exclusion from Gospel labor. Euodia and Syntyche labored with Paul in the Gospel (verse 3). The body functions by every member doing their part (Ephesians 4:16). Women, men, prominent and obscure — all fellow-laborers, all recorded in the book of life.

Application: Thank a fellow-laborer today — someone who has partnered with you in the Gospel, even in small ways. And then find a conflict (like Euodia and Syntyche's) that you can help resolve in the Body. The unity of the Spirit is worth keeping (Ephesians 4:3).

Post 256 · Day 62-4: Colossians 4:3-4 – Open Door for the Word of the Mystery

Who This Is For: Grace-age believers in the Body of Christ seeking to understand and live Paul's Gospel rightly divided.
“Withal praying also for us, that God would open unto us a door of utterance, to speak the mystery of Christ, for which I am also in bonds: That I may make it manifest, as I ought to speak.” — Colossians 4:3-4 KJV

Paul — the apostle of the mystery, the most qualified preacher of his age — prayed for an open door and for the ability to speak clearly. He did not presume on his own ability. He requested prayer. Even the greatest minister of the grace age acknowledged his dependence on God for every opportunity and every word.

Right Division: Right Division: 'The mystery of Christ' is Paul's specific focus — the Body of Christ, Jew and Gentile equal in one body. This is what needed the open door. The Kingdom Gospel was public, backed by miracles and national signs. The mystery Gospel went into the darkness of Gentile ignorance with no national backing, no signs confirming it to unbelievers, only the power of the Word and Spirit.

Application: Add 'an open door for the mystery' to your daily prayers. Pray specifically that God would open conversations where you can share what the grace age means — the mystery, the Body of Christ, the unsearchable riches. And pray for clarity: that you would make it manifest as you ought to speak.

Post 257 · Day 63-1: 1 Timothy 1:11 – The Glorious Gospel Committed to My Trust

Who This Is For: Grace-age believers in the Body of Christ seeking to understand and live Paul's Gospel rightly divided.
“According to the glorious gospel of the blessed God, which was committed to my trust.” — 1 Timothy 1:11 KJV

The Gospel is glorious. Not just good — glorious. It radiates the splendor of God. And this glorious Gospel was committed to Paul's trust. Trust implies faithfulness. A trustee does not modify the deposit — he guards and transmits it intact.

Right Division: Right Division: The glorious Gospel is Paul's Gospel of grace — not law, not mixture, not Kingdom. It is 'of the blessed God' — originating in God's own character of grace and blessing. The glory of the grace Gospel is that it asks nothing of the recipient but faith, and gives everything that God Himself possesses. This is what the glorious Gospel means: God's own riches, given freely, through faith alone in Christ alone.

Application: Spend time today simply marveling at the Gospel. Not analyzing it, not explaining it — just being astonished by it. A God who requires nothing from you but faith in His Son, and gives you everything in return. Let that glory settle over you today.

Post 258 · Day 63-2: Philemon 1:6 – Effective Faith Through Acknowledging Good

Who This Is For: Grace-age believers in the Body of Christ seeking to understand and live Paul's Gospel rightly divided.
“That the communication of thy faith may become effectual by the acknowledging of every good thing which is in you in Christ Jesus.” — Philemon 1:6 KJV

The effectiveness of sharing faith is tied to acknowledging what is already in you in Christ. Before you can communicate your faith effectively, you must know what you possess. The believer who knows their riches in Christ speaks with a confidence that the believer focused on their failures cannot.

Right Division: Right Division: 'Every good thing which is in you in Christ Jesus' — this is the grace-age identity. In the Law system, the focus was on what you must do to maintain standing. In grace, the focus is on what you already have in Christ. You are blessed with all spiritual blessings in heavenly places (Ephesians 1:3). Acknowledging these transforms your witness from duty to overflow.

Application: Make a list of every good thing in you in Christ Jesus. Draw from Ephesians 1:3-14, Colossians 1:12-14, Romans 8:28-39. Then share one of these with someone today — not as evangelism but as testimony. Let the abundance of what you have overflow in conversation.

Post 259 · Day 63-3: Romans 1:9 – Serving God in My Spirit in the Gospel

Who This Is For: Grace-age believers in the Body of Christ seeking to understand and live Paul's Gospel rightly divided.
“For God is my witness, whom I serve with my spirit in the gospel of his Son, that without ceasing I make mention of you always in my prayers.” — Romans 1:9 KJV

Paul's service was spiritual — not ceremonial, not tied to physical temple, not through a Levitical priesthood. He served God in his spirit in the Gospel. And this spiritual service expressed itself in ceaseless prayer for others. The inner life fueled the outward ministry.

Right Division: Right Division: 'Whom I serve with my spirit' marks the grace-age form of worship. Israel's service was tied to the temple, Levitical rites, and physical ordinances. The grace-age believer is a spiritual priest — no temple needed, no human mediator required, service rendered directly to God through the spirit. Romans 12:1 describes this: presenting your body as a living sacrifice is your 'reasonable service.'

Application: Pray without ceasing for someone in your sphere of ministry today. Set a reminder every few hours to lift one person to God in prayer. Paul's ceaseless intercession for the churches was the invisible engine of the visible fruit. Let your inner prayer life fuel your outer ministry.

Who This Is For: Grace-age believers in the Body of Christ seeking to understand and live Paul's Gospel rightly divided.

Week 9 has established the pattern for grace-age ministry: chosen vessel, Gospel proclamation, inner compulsion, cross-centered preaching, adaptable method, the mystery committed to trust, the door of utterance, co-labor, multiplication of faithful disciples. This is the template Paul modeled and commissioned Timothy to replicate.

Right Division: Right Division: Ministry in the grace age follows Paul's apostolic pattern — not the Kingdom program's signs and wonders, not the Levitical priesthood's ceremonies, but the Word of the mystery preached to all without distinction. Every believer is a minister of reconciliation (2 Corinthians 5:18). Every believer is a fellow-laborer (Philippians 4:3). The entire Body functions as a missionary unit.

Application: Review your notes from Week 9. Which truth hit you most? Which call to action did you actually take? Write down one specific way your ministry approach has shifted or deepened this week.
