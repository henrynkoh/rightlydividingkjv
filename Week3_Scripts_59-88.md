## Script 59 – Matthew 16:19 – Keys of the Kingdom

**Title:** Matthew 16:19 – Keys of the Kingdom: What Are They? | KJV Right Division
**Thumbnail:** "Key on open Bible, 'Keys of the Kingdom' white bold, 'Matthew 16:19 KJV' gold"

---

Hello, dear friends, and welcome. Today we open to one of the most debated verses in all of Scripture.

*"And I will give unto thee the keys of the kingdom of heaven: and whatsoever thou shalt bind on earth shall be bound in heaven: and whatsoever thou shalt loose on earth shall be loosed in heaven."* — Matthew 16:19 KJV

Jesus spoke these words to Peter, and they have been used to justify enormous claims — that Peter was the first pope, that the Church has authority to bind and loose spiritual powers, that earthly institutions govern heavenly outcomes. But right division gives us clarity.

**Right Division:** Jesus was speaking in the context of the Kingdom of Heaven — the earthly, prophetic Kingdom promised to Israel. The keys of the kingdom were an authority given to Peter specifically, in the context of Israel's program. Peter used these keys at Pentecost in Acts 2 — opening the Kingdom offer to Israel. He used them again in Acts 10 — opening the door to the Gentiles in a transitional moment.

This is not the authority of the Church, the Body of Christ. The Body of Christ is not a kingdom in the same sense — it is a mystery, revealed to Paul. We are not binding and loosing earthly kingdoms. Our calling is heavenly — Ephesians 1:3 — "blessed with all spiritual blessings in heavenly places in Christ."

The keys of the Kingdom were Peter's to use in his apostolic ministry to Israel and the transitional period of Acts. They were not handed to a religious institution. They were not passed down through papal succession. They were used — and the door is now open.

**Application:** Friend, you do not need a pope or priest to unlock heaven for you. The door has already been opened. Christ is the door — John 10:9 — "I am the door: by me if any man enter in, he shall be saved." Walk through it today by faith. No key needed — just trust in the One who opened the way.

## Script 60 – Ephesians 3:1-6 – The Mystery Revealed to Paul

**Title:** Ephesians 3:1-6 – The Mystery Hidden for Ages, Revealed to Paul | KJV
**Thumbnail:** "Sealed scroll opened, light pouring out, 'Mystery Revealed' white bold, 'Eph 3:1-6 KJV' gold"

---

Hello, beloved friends. Today we come to the heart of why Paul's ministry is so uniquely important for us today.

*"How that by revelation he made known unto me the mystery; (as I wrote afore in few words, Whereby, when ye read, ye may understand my knowledge in the mystery of Christ) Which in other ages was not made known unto the sons of men, as it is now revealed unto his holy apostles and prophets by the Spirit; That the Gentiles should be fellowheirs, and of the same body, and partakers of his promise in Christ by the gospel."* — Ephesians 3:3-6 KJV

**Right Division:** Three things stand out in these verses that are absolutely foundational. First — it was a mystery. The word mystery in Scripture does not mean something mysterious or confusing. It means a secret that was previously hidden but is now revealed. The Body of Christ — Jew and Gentile together as one — was not revealed in the Old Testament. It was hidden.

Second — it was not made known in other ages. This means the Old Testament prophets did not teach this. They prophesied about Israel's kingdom, Israel's Messiah, Israel's restoration. But the Church, the Body of Christ? Hidden. Kept secret until God's appointed time.

Third — it was revealed to Paul. Not to Peter, not to John, not to James. God specifically chose Paul — the apostle to the Gentiles — to be the revealer of this glorious mystery. Galatians 1:12 confirms: "I neither received it of man, neither was I taught it, but by the revelation of Jesus Christ."

The content of the mystery: Gentiles are now fellow heirs, of the same body, partakers of the promise — not through Israel's program, but directly through the Gospel of Grace.

**Application:** Friend, this mystery is your calling. You are not a second-class citizen who got into God's family through Israel's back door. You are a full heir — equal in Christ to every Jewish believer. That is breathtaking grace.

## Script 61 – Israel vs the Church: Two Distinct Programs

**Title:** Israel vs the Church – Two Distinct Programs in God's Plan | KJV Right Division
**Thumbnail:** "Star of David beside Cross, 'Two Distinct Programs' white bold, 'Eph 3:6 / Matt 16:18 KJV'"

---

Hello, dear friends. Today we tackle one of the most important distinctions in all of Scripture — the difference between Israel and the Church.

*"Even the mystery which hath been hid from ages and from generations, but now is made manifest to his saints."* — Colossians 1:26 KJV

*"And I say also unto thee, That thou art Peter, and upon this rock I will build my church; and the gates of hell shall not prevail against it."* — Matthew 16:18 KJV

**Right Division:** Israel and the Church are two distinct groups in God's eternal plan — with different callings, different covenants, different promises, and different futures. Confusing them is the root of most theological confusion in Christianity today.

Israel was chosen as God's earthly covenant people. Their promises are primarily earthly — land, kingdom, national blessing. Their covenant is rooted in Abraham, Moses, and David. Their hope is the literal restoration of the Kingdom to Israel — Acts 1:6. Their future includes the Great Tribulation, national repentance, and the Millennial Kingdom.

The Church — the Body of Christ — was not formed until Paul. It is a heavenly body. Its blessings are "in heavenly places" — Ephesians 1:3. Its calling is heavenly — Philippians 3:20. Its destiny is to be with Christ in glory. Its covenant is the grace covenant revealed through Paul's epistles.

They are not the same. They are not competing. They are two distinct expressions of God's purpose — one earthly, one heavenly. Both are glorious. Both are of grace. But they must not be mixed.

When you apply Israel's promises to the Church — tithing, land covenants, kingdom authority, dietary laws — you create confusion. When you apply Paul's Church truth to Israel — you strip Israel of her unique calling and prophetic future.

**Application:** Friend, knowing you are part of the Body of Christ — not a spiritual Israel, but a distinct heavenly body — gives you clarity, identity, and peace. You know who you are, what you have, and where you are going.

## Script 62 – Romans 11 – Grafting and the Future of Israel

**Title:** Romans 11 – Israel's Future and the Grafting of Gentiles | KJV
**Thumbnail:** "Olive tree with wild branch grafted in, 'Israel's Future' white bold, 'Romans 11 KJV' gold"

---

Hello, dear friends. Today we look at one of the most beautiful chapters in all of Paul's writings — Romans 11 — and Israel's extraordinary future.

*"I say then, Hath God cast away his people? God forbid."* — Romans 11:1 KJV

*"And so all Israel shall be saved: as it is written, There shall come out of Sion the Deliverer, and shall turn away ungodliness from Jacob."* — Romans 11:26 KJV

**Right Division:** Romans 11 gives us Paul's definitive answer to a crucial question — has God abandoned Israel? The answer is absolutely not. God forbid. God's promises to Israel are irrevocable — verse 29: "the gifts and calling of God are without repentance."

Paul uses the illustration of an olive tree. The natural branches — Israel — were broken off because of unbelief. The wild branches — Gentile believers — were grafted in through faith. But the tree belongs to Israel, and those natural branches will be grafted back in — verse 23: "if they abide not still in unbelief" they will be grafted in again.

This is dispensational truth at its most clear. God has not replaced Israel with the Church. He has temporarily set aside Israel as a nation — not forever, but for a purpose. During this parenthesis — the age of grace — Gentiles are being saved in remarkable numbers. But when the fullness of the Gentiles is complete — verse 25 — Israel's blindness will be lifted and all Israel will be saved.

What a day that will be! A nation born in a day. Zechariah 12:10 — Israel looking on the One they pierced and mourning. Then the Kingdom begins.

**Application:** Friend, understanding Romans 11 gives you love for Israel — not contempt, not replacement theology, but genuine love and anticipation. The people of God have a glorious future. And so do you — part of this wild olive branch, grafted in by grace.

## Script 63 – Acts 7 – The Transition Period

**Title:** Acts 7 – Stephen's Sermon and the Great Transition | KJV Right Division
**Thumbnail:** "Stephen looking up to heaven, 'The Great Transition' white bold, 'Acts 7 KJV' gold"

---

Hello, dear friends. Today we look at a pivotal chapter in Acts — the stoning of Stephen — and what it reveals about God's dispensational timetable.

*"But he, being full of the Holy Ghost, looked up stedfastly into heaven, and saw the glory of God, and Jesus standing on the right hand of God."* — Acts 7:55 KJV

Notice something remarkable — Jesus is standing. Everywhere else in the New Testament after His ascension, Jesus is seated at the right hand of the Father. Hebrews 10:12 — "sat down on the right hand of God." But at Stephen's martyrdom, He stands. As if He is rising to welcome this faithful witness — and to mark this moment.

**Right Division:** Acts is a transition book. It records the shift from the Kingdom program centered on Israel to the Grace program centered on the Body of Christ revealed through Paul. Acts 7 is a watershed moment in that transition.

Stephen reviews Israel's entire history in his sermon — from Abraham through Moses, through the tabernacle, to Solomon's temple. His point: Israel has consistently rejected God's messengers. He climaxes in verse 51 — "Ye stiffnecked and uncircumcised in heart and ears, ye do always resist the Holy Ghost." Israel's religious leadership is given their final opportunity and they refuse.

Their response? They stone him. And there, holding the coats of those who threw the stones, stands a young man named Saul of Tarsus — Acts 7:58. The man who would become Paul. The man to whom God would reveal the mystery of the Body of Christ. The transition is beginning.

Stephen's death marks the end of Israel's national opportunity. Within chapters, Saul is converted and the age of grace is launched in full.

**Application:** Friend, every page of Acts shows you a God who is patient, purposeful, and sovereign over history. No rejection of His messengers surprises Him. His plan was never in danger. And that same sovereignty watches over your life today.

## Script 64 – Kingdom Parables: Earthly or Heavenly?

**Title:** Kingdom Parables – Earthly Kingdom or Heavenly Mystery? | KJV Right Division
**Thumbnail:** "Mustard seed vs treasure chest, 'Kingdom Parables Explained' white bold, 'Matthew 13 KJV'"

---

Hello, dear friends. Today we look at the Kingdom parables of Matthew 13 and ask an important question — who are these parables for, and what do they teach?

*"The kingdom of heaven is like to a grain of mustard seed, which a man took, and sowed in his field."* — Matthew 13:31 KJV

*"And he said unto them, Unto you it is given to know the mystery of the kingdom of God: but unto them that are without, all these things are done in parables."* — Mark 4:11 KJV

**Right Division:** Jesus taught in parables about the Kingdom of Heaven — and He distinguished between two groups: His disciples who were given to know the mysteries of the Kingdom, and the crowds who received parables without full explanation. This itself is a dispensational distinction.

The Kingdom parables of Matthew 13 — the sower, the wheat and tares, the mustard seed, the leaven, the hidden treasure, the pearl, the net — all describe the condition of the Kingdom of Heaven during a time when the King is rejected and absent. These parables describe the current age from the perspective of the Kingdom program — how the Kingdom exists in mystery form while the King is away.

These are not Church truth — they are Kingdom truth. They describe the mixed condition of the world during the time between Israel's rejection of Messiah and His return to establish the literal Kingdom. The Church is not the Kingdom. The Church is a distinct heavenly body revealed to Paul.

But notice — even in the Kingdom parables, there is treasure, there is pearl, there is great value. The Kingdom of Heaven — in all its forms — is worth everything.

**Application:** Friend, when you read the parables, understand their context. Ask: Is Jesus speaking Kingdom truth or Grace truth? Is this Israel's program or the Body of Christ? Right division makes the parables come alive with clarity and beauty.

## Script 65 – The Church: Body of Christ (Ephesians 1:22-23)

**Title:** The Church as the Body of Christ – Ephesians 1:22-23 KJV
**Thumbnail:** "Human body silhouette with cross at center, 'Body of Christ' white bold, 'Eph 1:22-23 KJV' gold"

---

Hello, beloved friends. Today we look at one of the most magnificent descriptions of the Church in all of Scripture.

*"And hath put all things under his feet, and gave him to be the head over all things to the church, Which is his body, the fulness of him that filleth all in all."* — Ephesians 1:22-23 KJV

The Church is the Body of Christ. Christ is the Head. And together, this Body is described as His fullness — the fulness of him that filleth all in all.

**Right Division:** This is not the Church as an institution or a building or a religious organization. This is the Church as a living organism — a body with Christ as its Head. And this concept — the Body of Christ — was the heart of the mystery revealed to Paul.

In the Old Testament, Israel was the congregation of God. But the Body of Christ? Hidden. Revealed through Paul in Ephesians, Colossians, Romans, and Corinthians. This Body is comprised of all who are saved during the age of grace — Jew and Gentile, slave and free, male and female — all equally placed "in Christ" by the baptism of the Spirit — First Corinthians 12:13.

What does it mean to be the Body of Christ? First — we are organically connected to the Head. Christ does not govern this Body from a distance. He is the life of it — Colossians 3:4 — "Christ, who is our life." Second — we are connected to each other. Every member matters. First Corinthians 12:27 — "Now ye are the body of Christ, and members in particular." Third — we represent Christ to the world. Where He is, we are. What He does, He does through us.

**Application:** Friend, you are not just a church attender. You are a member of the Body of Christ — joined to the Head, joined to every other member, filled with His life. That is your identity. Live it.

## Script 66 – Bride vs Body: Two Distinct Descriptions

**Title:** Bride of Christ vs Body of Christ – What's the Difference? | KJV Right Division
**Thumbnail:** "Wedding ring beside body diagram, 'Bride vs Body' white bold, 'Eph 5 / Rev 21 KJV'"

---

Hello, dear friends. Today we explore two beautiful images for God's people — the Bride of Christ and the Body of Christ — and ask whether they are the same.

*"For the husband is the head of the wife, even as Christ is the head of the church: and he is the saviour of the body."* — Ephesians 5:23 KJV

*"And I John saw the holy city, new Jerusalem, coming down from God out of heaven, prepared as a bride adorned for her husband."* — Revelation 21:2 KJV

**Right Division:** Are the Bride and the Body the same group? This is a question that requires careful right division. Some teachers say yes — the Church is both the Body and the Bride of Christ. Others, through careful dispensational study, see distinctions.

Ephesians 5 uses the marriage analogy to describe the relationship between Christ and the Church — His Body. The Church as Body is compared to a wife who submits to her husband, and a husband who loves his wife as himself. This is a present-tense reality — we are currently united to Christ as Head.

Revelation 21's Bride — the New Jerusalem — appears in a future, prophetic context. The city itself is the bride. This may refer to the totality of the redeemed of all ages in their ultimate, glorified state — not limited to the Church age alone.

The key insight: whether the Bride equals the Body or represents something broader, both images speak to the intimacy, the love, and the eternal union between Christ and His people. Marriage is the deepest human covenant — and God uses it to describe His commitment to His own.

**Application:** Friend, Christ loves you with a love deeper than any human marriage can illustrate. He gave Himself for you. He is preparing a place for you. He is coming to receive you to Himself. That is your eternal future.

## Script 67 – Great Commission: Matthew 28 vs Paul's Commission

**Title:** Great Commission – Matthew 28 vs Paul's Commission | KJV Right Division
**Thumbnail:** "World map split between Israel flag and globe, 'Two Commissions' white bold, 'Matt 28 / Gal 1:16 KJV'"

---

Hello, dear friends. The Great Commission of Matthew 28 is one of the most familiar passages in Christianity. But right division asks — is this our commission today?

*"Go ye therefore, and teach all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Ghost: Teaching them to observe all things whatsoever I have commanded you."* — Matthew 28:19-20 KJV

*"To reveal his Son in me, that I might preach him among the heathen."* — Galatians 1:16 KJV

**Right Division:** Matthew 28 was given to the Eleven — the remaining apostles of Israel's program. The commission includes baptizing in the Trinitarian formula and teaching all nations to observe what Jesus commanded — which includes the Sermon on the Mount, the Kingdom ethics, and all of His earthly teaching.

Paul's commission was different. His was to preach the mystery — the Gospel of the grace of God — to the Gentiles, without water baptism as a requirement, without Kingdom ethics as the framework, but with the revelation of the Body of Christ.

This is not to say Matthew 28 has no value for us — it is glorious. But its full program awaits Israel's restoration. The 144,000 of Revelation, Jewish missionaries during the Tribulation, will fulfill Matthew 28 globally in a way the Church age never has.

For us today, Paul's commission applies — Second Timothy 2:2: "And the things that thou hast heard of me among many witnesses, the same commit thou to faithful men, who shall be able to teach others also." Preach the Gospel of Grace. Make disciples. Pass on the mystery.

**Application:** Friend, you have a commission — to share the Gospel of Grace, the death, burial, and resurrection of Christ, with everyone around you. Not complicated. Not conditional. Just the glorious truth of what Christ has done.

## Script 68 – Signs, Wonders, Miracles: For Which Dispensation?

**Title:** Signs and Wonders – Which Dispensation Are They For? | KJV Right Division
**Thumbnail:** "Miracles signs in background fading, 'Which Dispensation?' white bold, '2 Cor 12:12 KJV' gold"

---

Hello, dear friends. Today we address a question that touches every charismatic and Pentecostal discussion — are signs and wonders for today?

*"Truly the signs of an apostle were wrought among you in all patience, in signs, and wonders, and mighty deeds."* — 2 Corinthians 12:12 KJV

*"For the Jews require a sign, and the Greeks seek after wisdom: But we preach Christ crucified."* — 1 Corinthians 1:22-23 KJV

**Right Division:** Signs, wonders, and mighty deeds were specifically described as the signs of an apostle. They were given to authenticate the apostolic message during the transition period of Acts — when both Israel's Kingdom program and the Grace program were running simultaneously.

First Corinthians 1:22 makes a stunning dispensational statement — the Jews require a sign. Signs were given to Israel. Isaiah 8:18, Matthew 12:38-39, Hebrews 2:3-4 all connect the sign gifts to the Jewish audience and the Kingdom offer. When Israel rejected the Kingdom, the sign gifts served a transitional authenticating role.

Paul himself moved away from signs as his ministry matured. He did not heal Epaphroditus — Philippians 2:27. He left Trophimus sick at Miletus — Second Timothy 4:20. He told Timothy to take a little wine for his stomach — First Timothy 5:23. These are not the actions of someone still operating in the full sign-gift dispensation.

As the canon of Scripture was completed and the apostolic period closed, the signs that authenticated the apostles served their purpose. Second Timothy 3:16-17 now tells us Scripture itself is sufficient for doctrine, reproof, correction, and instruction.

**Application:** Friend, God still heals. God still moves in power. But we do not build our faith on signs — we build it on the Word. The completed Scripture is our authority and our sufficiency for this age.

## Script 69 – Tongues: Sign for Unbelieving Israel (1 Corinthians 14:22)

**Title:** Tongues – A Sign for Unbelieving Israel | 1 Corinthians 14:22 KJV
**Thumbnail:** "Map of Israel with speech bubbles, 'Tongues for Israel' white bold, '1 Cor 14:22 KJV' gold"

---

Hello, dear friends. Today we look at one of the most debated gifts in the Church — the gift of tongues — and what Paul says about its purpose.

*"Wherefore tongues are for a sign, not to them that believe, but to them that believe not."* — 1 Corinthians 14:22 KJV

And he cites the Old Testament precedent from Isaiah 28:11 — "With men of other tongues and other lips will I speak unto this people." The people referred to is Israel.

**Right Division:** Tongues were a covenant sign directed at Israel. Isaiah 28 prophecied that foreign tongues would be a sign of God's judgment and transition regarding the nation of Israel. At Pentecost in Acts 2, Jewish people from every nation heard the Gospel in their own language — a miraculous sign of the Gospel going out to the diaspora of Israel.

The sign gift of tongues told Israel — God is speaking to you through new means. The Kingdom offer is going out. Your Messiah has come. This is the sign Paul references.

First Corinthians 14 also gives us the context of tongues in the Corinthian church — a chaotic, immature, disorderly use of the gift. Paul does not celebrate this. He corrects it extensively, saying: "I had rather speak five words with my understanding, that by my voice I might teach others also, than ten thousand words in an unknown tongue." — verse 19.

Paul himself spoke in tongues more than them all — verse 18. But he explicitly ordered that in the church, all tongues must be interpreted, and if there is no interpreter, be silent. This is not the picture of uncontrolled ecstatic utterance that characterizes much of today's charismatic worship.

**Application:** Friend, your communication with God does not depend on a gift of tongues. You have the Spirit — Romans 8:26-27 — who intercedes for you with groanings that cannot be uttered. You have access to the Father through Christ. That is sufficient and glorious.

## Script 70 – Healing in the Kingdom vs Grace

**Title:** Healing in the Kingdom vs the Age of Grace | KJV Right Division
**Thumbnail:** "Jesus healing the sick vs Paul and sick Timothy, 'Two Dispensations' white bold, 'KJV'"

---

Hello, dear friends. Today we look at healing in Scripture — and why right division is essential to understanding it properly.

*"And Jesus went about all Galilee, teaching in their synagogues, and preaching the gospel of the kingdom, and healing all manner of sickness and all manner of disease among the people."* — Matthew 4:23 KJV

*"Drink no longer water, but use a little wine for thy stomach's sake and thine often infirmities."* — 1 Timothy 5:23 KJV

**Right Division:** During Jesus's earthly ministry and the early chapters of Acts, healing was widespread, spectacular, and immediate. Every disease was healed — blind eyes opened, lepers cleansed, dead raised. This was the King demonstrating the power of the Kingdom — Isaiah 35:5-6 had prophesied exactly these Kingdom signs.

But as we move through Acts into Paul's later epistles, the pattern changes dramatically. Paul, who performed great miracles in the early Acts period — Acts 14:10, Acts 19:11 — by the end of his ministry left Trophimus sick, advised Timothy about ongoing stomach problems, and prayed for Epaphroditus who recovered slowly and nearly died.

This is not inconsistency — it is dispensational development. The Kingdom healing signs served their authenticating purpose. As the mystery program of grace took center stage, Paul's emphasis shifted to the sufficiency of Christ in suffering — Second Corinthians 12:9 — "My grace is sufficient for thee: for my strength is made perfect in weakness."

God still heals today — sovereignly, miraculously, and graciously. He is the same God. But we do not have a guaranteed healing covenant in this age. Our covenant guarantee is grace, eternal life, and ultimate glorification.

**Application:** Friend, whether in health or illness, the grace of Christ is your portion. His strength is perfected in your weakness. That is a promise that never fails.

Lord Jesus, Your grace is truly sufficient. In our weakness, Your strength shines. We trust You. Amen.

## Script 71 – The Rapture: A Mystery (1 Thessalonians 4:16-17)

**Title:** The Rapture – A Mystery Paul Revealed | 1 Thessalonians 4:16-17 KJV
**Thumbnail:** "Clouds parting with figures ascending, 'Mystery of the Rapture' white bold, '1 Thess 4:16-17 KJV' gold"

---

Hello, dear friends. Today we come to one of the most thrilling truths in all of Scripture — the Rapture of the Church.

*"For the Lord himself shall descend from heaven with a shout, with the voice of the archangel, and with the trump of God: and the dead in Christ shall rise first: Then we which are alive and remain shall be caught up together with them in the clouds, to meet the Lord in the air: and so shall we ever be with the Lord."* — 1 Thessalonians 4:16-17 KJV

Caught up. The Latin translation — rapturo — gives us the word rapture. And it is a mystery revealed through Paul.

**Right Division:** First Corinthians 15:51-52 — "Behold, I shew you a mystery; We shall not all sleep, but we shall all be changed, In a moment, in the twinkling of an eye, at the last trump." Paul introduces this as a mystery — something not previously revealed in the Old Testament. The Old Testament saints knew of resurrection. But this specific event — the catching away of living believers to meet the Lord in the air — was hidden until Paul revealed it.

This distinguishes the Rapture from the Second Coming. The Rapture is for the Church, the Body of Christ — Christ comes for His own, in the air. The Second Coming is the return of Christ to earth — Zechariah 14:4 — "his feet shall stand in that day upon the mount of Olives." Two distinct events, separated by at least seven years.

The Rapture removes the Church before God's wrath falls on the earth — First Thessalonians 5:9 — "God hath not appointed us to wrath." The Tribulation is Daniel's 70th week — for Israel and the nations — not for the Body of Christ.

**Application:** Friend, the Rapture is not a scary doctrine — it is a comforting one. First Thessalonians 4:18 says — "Wherefore comfort one another with these words." Live ready. Live expectantly. He could come today.

## Script 72 – The Second Coming: Prophetic (Matthew 24)

**Title:** The Second Coming – Prophecy, Not Mystery | Matthew 24 KJV Right Division
**Thumbnail:** "Lightning across sky, 'Second Coming' white bold, 'Matthew 24 KJV' gold, dark storm background"

---

Hello, dear friends. Today we look at the Second Coming of Christ — and how it differs from the Rapture we studied yesterday.

*"For as the lightning cometh out of the east, and shineth even unto the west; so shall also the coming of the Son of man be."* — Matthew 24:27 KJV

*"And then shall appear the sign of the Son of man in heaven: and then shall all the tribes of the earth mourn, and they shall see the Son of man coming in the clouds of heaven with power and great glory."* — Matthew 24:30 KJV

**Right Division:** Matthew 24 is not about the Church. Read the context — Jesus is on the Mount of Olives, speaking to His Jewish disciples, answering their question about the destruction of the temple and the end of the age. This is prophetic truth for Israel.

The signs of Matthew 24 — wars, famines, earthquakes, false Christs, the abomination of desolation, the great tribulation — are the signs of the Day of the Lord and the Tribulation period. These are for Israel and the nations. The Church will not be present for these events — we will have been raptured before the Tribulation begins.

The Second Coming itself — which Matthew 24 describes — is Christ returning to earth, visibly, with power and great glory. Every eye shall see Him — Revelation 1:7. He comes to judge the nations — Matthew 25. He comes to deliver Israel — Zechariah 14. He comes to establish the Millennial Kingdom — Revelation 20.

The Rapture — caught up to meet Him in the air. The Second Coming — He comes down to earth. Two distinct events, two distinct purposes, two distinct groups.

**Application:** Friend, both events should fill you with joy. The Rapture is your personal homecoming. The Second Coming is the restoration of all things. God's plan is comprehensive, glorious, and certain.

## Script 73 – Tribulation Saints vs Church Age Believers

**Title:** Tribulation Saints vs Church Age Believers – What's the Difference? | KJV
**Thumbnail:** "Two groups separated by timeline, 'Tribulation vs Church Age' white bold, 'Rev 7 / Eph 2 KJV'"

Hebrews 12:22-23 tells us we have already come to "the heavenly Jerusalem, and to an innumerable company of angels, to the general assembly and church of the firstborn."

This heavenly citizenship reshapes everything. How we view money — not storing up earthly treasure. How we view suffering — light and momentary, working an eternal weight of glory. How we view death — to depart and be with Christ is far better. How we view relationships — knowing that in Christ, all earthly distinctions fade into something more glorious.

From heaven we look for our Saviour. The Rapture will be the homecoming of citizens returning to their native country.

**Application:** Friend, you are a stranger and pilgrim on this earth. This is not your home. Walk through this world holding its pleasures and pressures lightly — your home is somewhere far more wonderful, and your Saviour is coming to take you there.

## Script 88 – New Heavens and New Earth: Distinctions Matter

**Title:** New Heavens and New Earth – Israel's Earth or the Church's Heaven? | KJV Right Division
**Thumbnail:** "New Jerusalem descending, stars and earth, 'Eternal Distinctions' white bold, 'Rev 21 / Isa 65 KJV'"

---

Hello, dear friends. We come to our final message of Week 3, and we end by looking forward — all the way to eternity.

*"For, behold, I create new heavens and a new earth: and the former shall not be remembered, nor come into mind."* — Isaiah 65:17 KJV

*"And I saw a new heaven and a new earth: for the first heaven and the first earth were passed away."* — Revelation 21:1 KJV

Both Isaiah and John speak of new heavens and a new earth. But do right division distinctions apply even here, in eternity?

**Right Division:** Isaiah 65's prophecy of the new heavens and new earth is connected to Israel's restoration — with people still being born, living long lives, building houses, planting vineyards. This sounds like the Millennial Kingdom rather than the eternal state. The eternal state of Revelation 21-22 goes beyond even that — there is no more death, no more marriage, no more sea.

Revelation 21 describes the New Jerusalem descending from heaven — the eternal dwelling of the redeemed. The city is described in extraordinary dimensions and beauty. The nations walk in its light. The tree of life lines the river. God Himself is the temple and the light.

The question of where Israel and the Church dwell in eternity is debated among dispensational teachers. Some see the New Jerusalem as the heavenly home of the Church, with the nations of Israel and the redeemed of all ages dwelling on the new earth below. Others see all distinctions merged in eternity. What is certain is this: God has prepared something for you that is unimaginably glorious — First Corinthians 2:9 — "Eye hath not seen, nor ear heard, neither have entered into the heart of man, the things which God hath prepared for them that love him."

**Application:** Friend, whatever the eternal distinctions, you are going somewhere that surpasses all imagination. The same God who planned all of history with perfect precision has prepared your eternal home. Rest in that.
